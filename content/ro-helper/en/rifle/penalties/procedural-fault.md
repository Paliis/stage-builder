## What this is

A **procedural penalty** applies when the competitor breaches **procedure** for the **course of fire (COF)** or the **Written Stage Briefing (WSB)** without a more specific penalty clause in **Chapter 10** taking exclusive control. In **Rifle Jan 2026** the general framework is **10.1** (read **10.1.1–10.1.2** in your PDF). **Points per procedural**, per-shot vs per-occurrence counting, and caps are **only** as printed in **Rifle** — do not silently import **Handgun** numbers.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Capture the fact:** what happened, when in the **COF**, and how it relates to the **WSB**.
2. **Rule-check:** if a **specific** clause (movement, reload, position, etc.) applies, use that clause instead of a vague **10.1** label.
3. **Apply** the correct number of procedurals on the scoresheet per **10.1** + any more specific rule.
4. **Brief briefly** on the line; full debate goes through **9.6** / **RM**.
5. **Write auditable notes** (which WSB element, which shot or segment).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.1** — general procedural frame for **Rifle**; verify **10.1.2** point value and any special counting rules.
- **Procedural cap** (if any) — see **10.2.3** in **Rifle** PDF when relevant.

## Local (FPSU)

National variants may change scoring sheets — follow **MD** while staying consistent with the match’s IPSC tier. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Labelling** a specific breach (e.g. foot fault) as “generic 10.1” when **10.2.x** assigns the treatment.
- **Using Handgun** −10 from memory without opening **Rifle 10.1.2**.
- **Double penalties** for one act.
