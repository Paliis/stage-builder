## What this is

A **foot fault** occurs when the competitor, during the **COF**, contacts the ground **outside** the allowed area or crosses a **fault line** contrary to the **WSB** or general stage line rules. **Rifle Jan 2026** anchors this under **10.2.1** (see **10.2.1.1** for significant advantage while faulting, per your PDF). **Long gun** movement (kneeling, barricades, tight ports) can make line contact harder to see — still apply **Rifle** text, not **Handgun** layout assumptions.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record:** which line; which foot / boot contact; when in the **COF**; relation to shots if **10.2.1.1** may apply.
2. **Check the WSB:** whether the line is active for that position or movement segment.
3. **Apply** the scoresheet per **10.2.1** / **10.2.1.1** after PDF verification — do not relabel as generic **10.1** when **10.2.1** governs.
4. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
5. **If visibility is poor**, involve **CRO** / **RM** rather than guessing.

## Common mistakes

- **Wrong bucket:** use **10.2.1**, not vague **10.1**, when the foot-fault clause fits.
- **Handgun geometry** assumptions for **rifle** barricade foot placement.
- **Vague scoresheet** entries without shot / side / line ID.
