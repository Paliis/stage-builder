## What this is

**Failure to engage (FTE)** means the competitor **does not** meet required engagement on a target the **COF** / **WSB** mandates, with **penalty** treatment under **10.2.7** and **scoring** treatment under **9.5.6** in **Rifle Jan 2026** — **confirm exact wording and tables in the Rifle PDF**; do not assume paragraph text is identical to **Handgun** even when numbers match.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** which target(s) were mandatory; shots fired; visible misses vs no-shoot decisions per **Chapter 9**.
2. **Check the WSB** for engagement order and minimum hits.
3. **Apply** scoring and procedural elements per **9.5.6** and **10.2.7** together after PDF verification.
4. **Brief briefly** on the line; challenges per **9.6**.
5. **Document** target ID, array, and shot sequence for audits.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.2.7** — penalty anchor for FTE-style faults in **Rifle** context.
- **9.5.6** — scoring anchor; read **Chapter 9** and **Chapter 10** together.

## Common mistakes

- **Handgun Chapter 9** memory instead of **Rifle** tables.
- **Applying only 10.2.7** without fixing score entries per **9.5.6**.
- **Vague scoresheet** — “FTE” with no target label.
