## What this is

**Rifle Jan 2026** defines **trigger pull** / **release force** limits for divisions that require checks, via **equipment rules and appendices** (the matrix reused **Handgun** planning notes — **do not** assume identical numbers). **Rifle** triggers, safeties, and gauge access points differ from **handguns** and from **PCC**. **Only** the **Rifle** PDF governs enforcement.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Treat** pull-weight checks as **equipment inspection** / **RM** territory unless **MD** assigns bay duties with proper tools.
2. **Refuse** to guess pass/fail; direct the competitor to the authorised **Rifle** procedure.
3. **Stop** the line for gross mechanical unsafety (e.g. trigger with safety engaged) — **RM**.
4. **Do not** cite **Handgun D4 §6** kg values on a **Rifle** match without **Rifle** PDF confirmation.
5. **Escalate** disputes after measurement to **RM**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **Chapter 5** + **appendices** for **Rifle** divisions requiring trigger checks; record the verified appendix section after review.

## Local (FPSU)

National chrono / equipment teams may own all gauge checks — follow **MD**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Handgun Production** muscle memory applied to **Rifle** divisions.
- **Improvised** pull tests with non-calibrated tools.
- **Ignoring** that some **Rifle** divisions may **not** use the same pull rule set as **Production** handgun.
