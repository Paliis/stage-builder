## What this is

In **Rifle Jan 2026**, **10.5.11** governs **external (manual) safety** for **long guns during movement** — when the rifle’s safety must be **engaged/disengaged**, in which **course segments**, and what constitutes a **DQ**. The **same rule number** in **Handgun** addresses **trigger finger** behaviour, not manual safeties on a pistol. Always use the **Rifle** PDF for adjudication.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** the manual safety during **movement**, **transitions**, and **ready / unload** sequences as defined in **Chapter 8** and the **WSB**.
2. **Stop** the competitor on a clear **10.5.11** breach; involve **RM** for **DQ** when the rule and facts require it.
3. **Do not import** PCC stage habits unless the **Rifle** text matches — similar idea, **separate primary_url**.
4. **Separate** this from **trigger placement** faults that may fall under other **10.5.x** clauses in **Rifle**.
5. **On mixed bays**, explicitly warn squads which discipline’s **10.5.11** applies.

## Common mistakes

- **Using Handgun** memory anchors for **Rifle** long-gun movement.
- **Missing** a safety disengaged while the competitor is still moving between positions.
- **Debating** “my rifle has no manual safety” without checking division / equipment appendix rules.
