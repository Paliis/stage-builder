## What this is

**Chapter 10.5** **Rifle** sets **trigger-finger discipline** for the **COF**: finger **outside** the guard during movement, certain reloads, barricade work, and when not engaging, as **written in the Rifle PDF**. Matrix shorthand **10.5.9–10.5.11** follows **Handgun** — **verify** the exact **sub-rules** in **IPSC Rifle Competition Rules** **Jan 2026**. See **C115** (handgun) for topic parallel only.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Line of sight** to trigger guard from the RO box; use a second official on awkward stages.
2. **Phase:** prone vs. standing vs. port — the competitor may mask the guard; **do not** call without evidence.
3. **Stop / RM** per match protocol for **DQ**-level breaches.
4. **Write down** position, support hand, and whether the rifle was shouldered.
5. **Equipment** (gloves, wide triggers) does not change the rule — only the **PDF** test.

## Common mistakes

- **Judging from muzzle** direction only — the **finger** rule is separate from **10.5.2** (180°).
- **Calling “DQ”** when only **procedural** fault is clear — different chapters.
