## What this is

**10.5.14** in **Rifle Jan 2026** addresses **ammunition** in the **Safety Area** — usually **DQ** for the same safety reasons as other IPSC disciplines. **Rifle** bays often have **larger bags**, **magazines**, and **loose rounds** near the cleaning / adjustment zone; the official text still defines what counts as **ammunition** and any **exceptions**. Read the **Rifle** PDF, not **Handgun** or **PCC**, for the binding wording.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** that live rounds do not appear on the **Safety Area** table, mat, or competitor bags during gun handling.
2. **Stop** the breach verbally; if the competitor refuses to clear it — **RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.14** after confirmation.
3. **Do not grab** someone else’s ammo — direct the owner; if unsafe — **CEASE FIRE** per protocol.
4. **Brief newcomers** on where the **Safety Area** boundary is; rifle stages may stretch the bay physically but not the rules.
5. **Do not confuse** with allowed **empty magazine** rules if the PDF separates them.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

## Common mistakes

- **Assuming** identical exceptions to **Handgun** without opening **Rifle** rules.
- **RO carries** ammo while entering the gun-service zone.
- **Ignoring** a breach to avoid awkwardness.
