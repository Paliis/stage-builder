## What this is

The **180° / muzzle safe angles** rule in **IPSC Rifle** keeps the muzzle **downrange** within the **safe sector** defined by the range, backstop, and **WSB**. Pointing past the safe boundary is a **critical safety violation**. For **handgun** / **PCC** anchors on the same rule number, see **C105** / **C106** — on rifle matches, use the **Rifle** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop:** Loud, clear **“STOP!”** (or match-standard cease-fire).
2. **Control:** Approach only if safe; competitor stops firing; rifle stays pointed in a **safe** direction.
3. **Unload / clear:** Per **Rifle** division and **WSB** (sling, bag, unload table, etc.).
4. **After safety:** Under **10.5.2**, a muzzle-safe-angle breach is normally **DQ** — confirm wording in **IPSC Rifle Competition Rules** (**Jan 2026**), **Chapter 10**. Competitor does not continue after that DQ.
5. **Report:** **RM**; scoresheet **“DQ — Rule 10.5.2 (180° / muzzle safe angles)”** with time and place.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Read **10.5.2** in **Rifle** for full criteria during **movement**, **reloads**, **malfunction work**, and **transitions**. **Uprange** stages and tight ports exaggerate muzzle arc — watch **WSB** flow and berms.

## Common mistakes

- **Tracking feet** instead of **muzzle line** when the competitor pivots.
- **Sling / barricade:** rifle is pushed sideways; muzzle crosses the plane while the body still “faces” targets.
- **Borrowing PCC/handgun intuition** without opening the **Rifle** PDF.
