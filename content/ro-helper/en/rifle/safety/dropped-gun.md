## What this is

A **dropped firearm** under **IPSC Rifle** covers the rifle **unintentionally released** so it falls / strikes the ground during the **COF** or as **10.5.3** describes. Barricades, slings, and bipods change *how* drops happen — the **test** is in **Rifle** **Jan 2026** **10.5.3**. **C120** is **handgun** only.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Cease fire**; **no** catching; secure the bay.
2. **Control access** until **RM** authorizes pickup and inspection (muzzle direction, bolt state).
3. **Note** whether a magazine or only the rifle fell — magazines may be **other** rules in the PDF.
4. **DQ** path per **10.5.3** + **RM** if regs require.
5. **Incident report** for insurance / MD if the range requires it.

## Common mistakes

- **Confusing** a controlled **unload** on a mat with an uncontrolled **drop** — read the rule text.
- **Allowing** the squad to move gear before the firearm is verified safe.
