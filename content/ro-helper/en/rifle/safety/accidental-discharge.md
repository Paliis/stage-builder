## What this is

An **accidental discharge (AD)** under **IPSC Rifle** is defined in **Section 10.4** of the **Jan 2026** **Rifle** PDF. A shot **outside** controlled fire on command during the **COF**, or other **enumerated** situations, normally leads to **DQ**; exceptions are **only** in the rulebook. Cross-discipline context: **C110** (handgun). Always apply **Rifle** **10.4** on rifle matches.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Cease fire / STOP** per MD; maintain distance; **muzzle** awareness with long guns.
2. **Freeze** competitor handling until **RM** if required.
3. **Document:** time, position (prone / barricade / port), bolt state if observed, command sequence, witnesses.
4. **DQ** per **10.4** after any required **RM** review.
5. **RM** handoff for incident package.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Full **10.4** text in **Rifle**; check cross-references (steel distance, etc., if cited in that edition).
- Do not use **Handgun** PDF for AD wording.

## Common mistakes

- **Labeling** a controlled **double** or rulebook **exception** as AD without **10.4** text.
- **Confusing** AD with **squib** / **malfunction** procedures — different sections.
