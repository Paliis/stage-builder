## What this is

**10.5.14** in **PCC Jan 2026** addresses **ammunition** (and related items per PDF wording) in the **Safety Area** — usually **DQ** because it increases **AD** risk and breaks zone discipline. With a **long gun**, magazines and loose rounds on a **service table** are a common flashpoint. **Confirm exceptions** (empty cases, dummy rounds, etc.) **only** in the **PCC** PDF — do not assume paragraph text is identical to **Handgun** word-for-word.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** that live rounds do not appear in the **Safety Area** during gun handling, including **PCC** on benches / racks if that is within the marked zone.
2. **Stop** the breach verbally; if the competitor refuses to clear it — **RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.14** after confirmation.
3. **Do not grab** someone else’s ammo — direct the owner; if unsafe — **CEASE FIRE** per protocol.
4. **Brief newcomers** where the **Safety Area** boundary is for **PCC** flow (often busier than handgun bays).
5. **Do not confuse** with allowed **empty magazine** rules if the PDF separates them.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.5.14** in **PCC**; read with **Chapter 2** (Safety Area) and **10.3** (DQ administration). Cross-check **Handgun** only for study, not as a substitute.

## Local (FPSU)

**Safety Area** markings must be visible; extra bans — **MD**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating PCC like handgun** on a mixed range — same **safety idea**, different **PDF**.
- **RO carries** ammo while entering the gun-service zone.
- **Ignoring** a breach to avoid awkwardness.
