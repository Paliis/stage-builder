## What this is

In **PCC Jan 2026**, **10.5.11** addresses **external (manual) safety** behaviour for **long guns during movement** — when the selector must be **on/off**, during **specific commands**, and what constitutes a **DQ**. This is **not** the same paragraph theme as **Handgun 10.5.11** (trigger finger / deactivation context in the handgun book). Always read **PCC** wording; for **handgun** bays see the **`trigger-finger`** card.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Observe** safety-selector position during **Make Ready**, **movement between arrays**, and **Unload / Show clear** per **WSB** + **Chapter 8** context in your PDF.
2. **Stop** the competitor if you see a clear breach of **10.5.11**; call **RM** for **DQ** if the rule mandates it after you confirm the fact pattern.
3. **Do not coach** “your division always does X” — only the published rule and **MD** briefing.
4. **Separate** this issue from **trigger finger** faults that may use different sub-rules in PCC.
5. **On shared ranges**, brief that **PCC** ≠ **handgun** for **10.5.11** numbering content.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.5.11** in **PCC**; read adjacent **10.5.x** safety sub-rules in the same PDF for related DQ context.

## Common mistakes

- **Opening Handgun** PDF to judge a **PCC** movement safety case.
- **Ignoring** a visibly disengaged manual safety during a required movement segment.
- **Arguing** selector ergonomics instead of applying the rule text.
