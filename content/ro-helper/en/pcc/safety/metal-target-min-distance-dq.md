## What this is

**10.4.7** in **PCC Jan 2026** covers **disqualification** when a competitor engages **steel** from closer than the distance set in that PDF (and any combined conditions ‚Äî **read the full sub-clause**). **C310** = **Handgun** ‚Äî **PCC** uses the **PCC** rulebook; **do not** assume the **handgun** metre value without checking. This is **critical** safety; **ROs** must know published match minimums and **not allow** a stage layout that forces a breach.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Check WSB and layout:** distances to poppers / plates vs **10.4.7** and **MD** briefing (PCC **long gun** geometry differs from handgun).
2. **Stop the COF** if mid-string it becomes clear the competitor **violated** minimum distance ‚Äî then **RM** for **DQ** under **10.4.7** (per PDF).
3. **Do not ‚Äúmeasure by eye‚Äù** for the official call ‚Äî involve **RM** / tools if the match defines a method.
4. **Warn MD** before the match if a stage is **impossible** without breaking **10.4.7**.
5. **Document** for the DQ record.

## Common mistakes

- **Using Handgun 10.4.7 numbers** for PCC without opening the **PCC** PDF.
- **Publishing** a stage that forces a shot inside the **PCC** limit.
- **Warning instead of DQ** when the PDF requires **10.4.7**.
