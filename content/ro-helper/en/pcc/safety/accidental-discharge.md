## What this is

An **accidental discharge (AD)** under **IPSC PCC** is defined in **Section 10.4** of the **Jan 2026** PCC PDF: a shot **outside** controlled fire on the **RO’s command** during the **COF**, or other situations **listed there**. Outcome is usually **DQ**; **exceptions** (e.g. **Safety Area**) are **only** per the official text. For **handgun** wording of the same topic, see **C110** — on PCC matches use the **PCC** book.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Safety first:** **“CEASE FIRE”** / **“STOP”** per protocol; **muzzle** control and safe distance (carbine handling differs from pistol — still **10.4** in PCC).
2. **Freeze handling** until **RM** / staff if the briefing requires it.
3. **Record facts:** time, bay, sling / ready position, RO command sequence, witnesses.
4. **DQ only per procedure** — **RM** confirmation if required; then **10.4** on the scoresheet.
5. **Hand off to RM** for documentation and follow-up.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.4** in **PCC**; read all sub-clauses and cross-references.
- Do not substitute **C110** (Handgun) text for sanction text — open **PCC** PDF.

## Common mistakes

- **Assuming** PCC **10.4** matches handgun line-for-line — verify **PCC** PDF.
- **No record** of whether the shot was during **COF** vs. other **10.4** cases.
- **RO** handles the competitor’s carbine after AD — follow match safety rules only.
