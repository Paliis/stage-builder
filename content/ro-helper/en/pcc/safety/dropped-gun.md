## What this is

A **dropped firearm** under **IPSC PCC** — the carbine **unintentionally released** and contacts the ground / deck during the **COF** or in other circumstances **defined in 10.5.3** — is normally **DQ**. Slings, bags, and table procedures are **PCC**-specific; read **10.5.3** in the **PCC** PDF (**Jan 2026**), not **Handgun** habits. For **handgun** wording, see **C120**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Do not catch** a falling long gun; **STOP** / **CEASE FIRE** per protocol; keep people clear of the muzzle arc.
2. **Freeze** the line until **RM** / staff control pickup and clear.
3. **Record:** sling failure vs. competitor release; whether the muzzle moved during the fall; any discharge (**10.4** cross-check).
4. **DQ** under **10.5.3** after **RM** confirmation if required.
5. **Photos / sketch** of where the firearm landed if props or berms are involved.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.5.3** in **PCC** **Section 10.5**; verify exceptions (e.g. controlled placement vs. drop) in that book.

## Common mistakes

- **Applying C120** (holster) logic to **PCC** — different handling rules.
- **Letting the competitor** re-shoulder before **RM** clears the scene.
