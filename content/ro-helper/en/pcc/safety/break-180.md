## What this is

The **180° / muzzle safe angles** principle in **IPSC PCC** is the same safety idea as for handgun: the muzzle must stay **downrange** within the **designated safe sector**. Crossing the boundary defined by the stage / backstop (or **WSB**) is a **critical safety violation**. For the **handgun** wording of the same anchor, see **C105** — always apply the **PCC** PDF on PCC matches.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop:** Immediately issue a loud, clear **“STOP!”** command.
2. **Control:** Approach the competitor only if safe; ensure they cease fire and keep the carbine pointed in a safe direction.
3. **Unload / clear:** Direct unload and show clear per **PCC** match procedures and division rules (sling, bag, table — per **WSB** / MD).
4. **End of COF:** After safety is verified, use the **approved** finish procedure (e.g. sling, case, unload table) per the rulebook and **WSB**. Under **10.5.2**, a muzzle-safe-angle breach is normally **DQ** — confirm exact wording in the official **IPSC PCC Competition Rules** PDF (**Jan 2026**), **Chapter 10**.
5. **Report:** Summon the **Range Master (RM)**. Record **“DQ — Rule 10.5.2 (180° / muzzle safe angles)”** on the **scoresheet**, with time and location.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Per **10.5.2** in **PCC**, read the full paragraph on muzzle direction during the **COF**, including **movement**, **malfunction clearance**, and **reloads**. Long-gun handling (sling transitions, shouldering) can make **uprange** stages especially sensitive — verify **WSB** berms and flow.

## Common mistakes

- **Judging from the competitor’s eyes** instead of **muzzle line** relative to the safe sector.
- **Reload / transition:** carbine rotates across the body and the muzzle **sweeps** past the 180° plane.
- **Assuming** PCC exceptions match **handgun** holster rules — confirm in the **PCC** PDF.
