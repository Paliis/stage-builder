## What this is

**Chapter 10.5** **PCC** defines when the **finger must stay outside the trigger guard** during the **COF** (movement, reload, not engaging, etc.). **10.5.9–10.5.11** in **Handgun** are the usual anchor labels in the matrix — **open the PCC PDF** and confirm **paragraph numbers** for **Jan 2026**; outcomes are typically **DQ**-level when the rule is breached, per that book. For **handgun** phrasing, see **C115**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Observe** the trigger guard from a legal RO position; if obscured — second official / **CRO**, not a guess.
2. **Map the phase:** movement, reload, transition to sling, aiming — match the correct **10.5.x** paragraph in **PCC**.
3. **Stop the COF** and escalate per MD if a **DQ**-level breach is clear; **RM** confirmation if required.
4. **Document** body position, carbine orientation, and RO commands around the breach.
5. **Apply consistently** — selective “warnings” without rule text undermine fairness.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **Ch.10.5** in **PCC** fully for trigger discipline; do not copy **Handgun** wording without checking **PCC**.

## Common mistakes

- **Using C115** sanction text instead of **PCC** **10.5.x**.
- **DQ** with no documented **COF phase** tied to the rule paragraph.
- **Poor sight line** to the trigger guard — weak evidence under protest.
