## What this is

**IPSC PCC** paper targets use **A / C / D** zones (see target artwork in **appendices**). **Major** vs **Minor** for the competitor’s division changes **multipliers** and penalty economics per **Chapter 9** tables in the **PCC Jan 2026** PDF. **C180** = **Handgun** — open **PCC** rules for PCC divisions.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Verify** the **target type** on the berm matches the **WSB** (mini, classic, etc.).
2. **Call the zone** per **9.5 PCC** — boundary lines from the PDF, not memory from handgun.
3. **Record** A/C/D (or match equivalent) as the **scoring** system expects.
4. **Do not** re-label **Major/Minor** on the line — **registration** / division; fixes via **scoring / RM**.
5. **Edge holes** — overlay / **CRO** per **MD** policy.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **9.5 PCC** + tables / appendices; align with **9.4 PCC** (values).

## Common mistakes

- **Assuming** handgun **zone dimensions** match **PCC** targets without checking **appendices**.
- **Incomplete** zone entries — **Major/Minor** cannot apply correctly downstream.
