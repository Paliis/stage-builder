## What this is

**10.2.12** in **IPSC PCC** addresses firing when the **fire control** or **discharge pattern** (e.g. **binary trigger**, **burst**, or **fully automatic** operation — **as defined in the PDF**) is **not permitted** for the competitor’s **division**, equipment rules, or **COF**. This card applies to **PCC** (and similar long-gun divisions that use **10.2.12** in their book — always open the **PCC** PDF). Outcomes (**procedural**, **DQ**, etc.) are **only** from **Jan 2026** text and match / division bulletins.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Confirm facts:** what was observed (multiple rounds per single deliberate pull, runaway burst, selector position, etc.) — **only** per match evidence policy and **safe** observation.
2. **Read 10.2.12** in **PCC Chapter 10** together with **Ch.5** equipment rules for the division.
3. **Apply the sanction** strictly per the PDF — do not substitute labels (“sounds like binary”) for rule text.
4. **Scoresheet / RM:** if classification or legality of the firearm is in doubt, escalate per match procedure.
5. **Safety first** if uncontrolled fire occurs — **RM** and range protocols before scoring debate.

## Common mistakes

- **Mixing disciplines:** **Shotgun** wrong-ammo uses a different **10.2.12** context — use the **PCC** book for **PCC**.
- **Calling a mode without evidence** or without a **PDF** basis.
- **RO adjusts selector** on a competitor’s firearm — never; only the competitor handles the carbine per commands.
