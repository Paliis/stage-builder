## What this is

**Failure to engage (FTE)** covers mandated targets not properly engaged per **COF** / **WSB**, with **penalty** under **10.2.7** and **scoring** under **9.5.6** in **PCC Jan 2026**. **PCC** arrays and paper/metal mixes resemble **Rifle** more than **Handgun** — still open **PCC** **Chapter 9** and **Chapter 10** together; do not adjudicate from another book.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** mandatory targets, shots, and scoring hits/misses per **WSB**.
2. **Resolve** no-shoot and penalty interactions using **PCC** tables, not memory from another discipline.
3. **Apply** **9.5.6** + **10.2.7** per verified PDF text.
4. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
5. **On mixed bays**, confirm the stage is scored as **PCC** before citing rules.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)


### 10.2.7 + 9.5.6 — FTE: per target + misses

- **FTE (Failure To Engage)**: penalty logic is **one procedural per target** under **10.2.7**.
- In addition, apply **Misses** for the un-hit required shots (scoring mechanics per **9.5.6** and your PDF tables).
- Net: **procedural per target + misses**, not “misses only” and not “procedural per shot”.

- **10.2.7** — penalty anchor for FTE-style faults in **PCC**.
- **9.5.6** — scoring anchor; joint read with **10.2.7**.
