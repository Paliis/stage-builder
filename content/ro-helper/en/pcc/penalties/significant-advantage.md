## What this is

**Significant advantage** is an **unfair material gain** during the **COF**—often by shooting from a position that improves target access beyond **WSB** limits. **PCC Jan 2026** uses **10.2.1.1** and **10.2.2** in **Chapter 10**. **PCC** combines **long gun** movement with **pistol-caliber** handling; advantage cases around ports, barricades, and transitions should be judged from **PCC**, not **Rifle** or **Handgun** memory alone.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** competitor position vs **WSB** boxes and movement strings.
2. **Escalate** interpretation doubts to **CRO** / **RM** before finalising significant advantage.
3. **Apply** **10.2.1.1** / **10.2.2** per PDF — avoid mis-routing to **10.1** when advantage clauses apply.
4. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
5. **Document** shots and WSB references.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.2.1.1**, **10.2.2** in **PCC** — confirm counting rules in your edition.

## Common mistakes

- **Rifle** PDF citations on a **PCC-only** stage (or vice versa).
- **Treating** every tight port shoot as “advantage” without **WSB** breach facts.
- **Double penalties** for one manoeuvre.
