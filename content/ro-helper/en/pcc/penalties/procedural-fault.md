## What this is

A **procedural penalty** applies for **COF** / **WSB** procedure breaches when **Chapter 10** does not give a more specific exclusive rule. **PCC Jan 2026** frames this under **10.1** (read **10.1.1–10.1.2**). **Long gun** stages can produce procedural issues around **movement**, **port loading**, and **strong/weak** handling — still map them to the **correct 10.2.x** clause when one exists before defaulting to **10.1**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record facts** with WSB references and timing in the **COF**.
2. **Prefer specific rules** (reload, position, finger, etc.) over a vague **10.1** label when the PDF assigns one.
3. **Apply** the scoresheet count per **10.1** + any specific clause.
4. **Keep the line moving** — challenges per **9.6**, not arguments.
5. **Document** clearly for **RM** review.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.1** — PCC general procedural frame; verify **10.1.2** point value.
- **Caps** — **10.2.3** when applicable in **PCC** PDF.

## Local (FPSU)

Match software may auto-sum procedurals — human RO must still choose the **correct rule anchor**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Handgun-only mental model** for **PCC** movement procedurals.
- **Wrong point value** because **Handgun** was memorised instead of **PCC** opened.
- **Double counting** the same breach.
