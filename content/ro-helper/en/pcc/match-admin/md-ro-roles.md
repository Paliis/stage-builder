## What this is

**Chapter 7** defines the **officials chain** for **PCC**: the **Match Director (MD)** runs the overall event; the **Range Master (RM)** owns safety, rule interpretation, and on-site decisions; the **Chief Range Officer (CRO)** coordinates bays; the **RO** runs the **COF** on their bay. An **RO** does **not** replace the **RM** on **DQ**, match structure, or post-publication **WSB** changes. **C285** = **Handgun** — use the **PCC** PDF for **Chapter 7**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Follow** lawful **RM** / **CRO** instructions immediately (cease fire, squad order changes).
2. **Escalate upward** when the issue leaves the bay: protests, DQ, stage rebuilds, major kit failures.
3. **Do not announce** **MD** decisions unless you **are** the **MD**.
4. **Log facts** neutrally for **RM** (time, commands, witnesses).
5. **Support** other ROs: concise handoffs without losing context.

## Common mistakes

- **RO refuses** to call **RM** “to avoid bothering them”.
- **Public argument** with **RM** on the bay.
- **Hiding incidents** the **MD** must know (injury, DQ).
- Reading **Handgun Ch.7** **without** opening the **PCC** PDF.
