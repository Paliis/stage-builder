## What this is

**PCC Jan 2026** defines **trigger pull weight** (or equivalent **release force**) limits for divisions that require it — typically via **equipment appendices** (the product matrix reused **Handgun Appendix D / D4 §6** and **2.27 kg** as a **planning anchor**). **PCC is a long gun**: measurement method, divisions, and numbers may **differ** from **Handgun**. **Confirm every value and sub-clause in the PCC PDF** before enforcing.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **On the bay**, do **not** certify trigger pull unless **MD** / **equipment staff** explicitly assigns that duty with proper tools.
2. **If asked** “does my PCC pass?”, answer: **only** the authorised **PCC** appendix check and **RM** outcome count.
3. **Hold the start** on obvious mechanical safety defects and call **RM** — separate from pull weight but urgent.
4. **Do not** reuse **Handgun** pull numbers or gauge technique without **PCC** verification.
5. **Escalate** post-measurement disputes to **RM**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Open **Chapter 5** + relevant **appendices** for **PCC** divisions requiring trigger checks; note the **exact** appendix and section after PDF review.

## Local (FPSU)

Who may operate gauges at Level I can vary — follow **MD** / national bulletin. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Quoting 2.27 kg** from **Handgun** memory on a **PCC** line.
- **Bay RO “approving”** trigger weight verbally.
- **Mixing** **Open** vs **Production** PCC configurations without reading division rules.
