## What this is

An **equipment malfunction** during a match is when the **PCC**, **magazine**, **optic**, or other allowed gear **fails** without competitor fault (bolt won’t close, trigger fails, mag body breaks, etc.). Under **PCC Jan 2026**, whether you **stop**, **reshoot**, or **score as-is** depends on the **exact PDF clause** (this card’s matrix cites **5.7** — **verify** whether that overlaps **PF** text or another paragraph on malfunctions / **Chapter 4** reshoots). **C265** = **Handgun** — use the **PCC** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop the COF** with **“STOP”** / **“CEASE FIRE”** if continuing is unsafe or impossible.
2. **Make the gun safe** per **Chapter 8** commands.
3. **Do not solo-declare** an “automatic reshoot” — only per PDF + **MD**.
4. **Log** what happened (failure type, shots before failure) for **RM** / scoring.
5. **After RM decision** — run the reshoot or enter the score as the rule requires.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read the PDF: **malfunction** vs **user error** vs **safety breach** — different outcomes.
- Cross-check **4.7** (if reshoots live there) and **9.10** (time).

## Common mistakes

- **Treating** a skill error as a **malfunction**.
- **Mercy reshoot** without a rule basis.
- **RO repairs** the carbine on the bay — not allowed; only the competitor in the **Safety Area** per rules.
- Using **Handgun** malfunction text **without** opening the **PCC** PDF.
