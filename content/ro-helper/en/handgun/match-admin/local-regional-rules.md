## What this is

**3.3** explains how the **Match Director (MD)** may add **local** or **regional** match conditions (spectator zones, medical add-ons, schedule quirks) without replacing **core IPSC rules**. **ROs** must follow the **published MD** briefing and must **not** enforce “local rules” that contradict **DQ**, **safety**, or **scoring** in the PDF without **RM** / IPSC authority.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read** the match addendum / **MD** bulletin before your first shift.
2. **Apply** only what is **published** and available to all competitors.
3. **On conflict** between a verbal instruction and IPSC — pause and go to **RM**.
4. **Do not invent** “range tradition” absent from the briefing.
5. **Point competitors** to where local rules are posted (site, registration pack).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **3.3**; boundary is **safety** and **fair play** in **Chapters 2, 9, 10**.

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU / UPSF layers must be **transparent** before the start. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Secret** locals only for “insiders”.
- **MD changes the COF** without updating the WSB.
- **RO ignores** a published MD rule because “not in IPSC” — **RM** first.
