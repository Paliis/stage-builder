## What this is

**Chapter 11** governs **protests** against official acts and **arbitration** (jury composition, procedure, appeals of jury rulings, etc.). This is **not** the same as **9.6** (target scoring challenges) — though a competitor may walk **multiple steps** in practice. An **RO** is **not** on the jury and does **not** issue match-level final rulings.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Explain the route:** where to file, by when, in what form — per **Chapter 11** and **MD**.
2. **Preserve evidence** (targets, sheet, video) per **RM**; do not alter the stage without permission.
3. **Do not pressure** a competitor to withdraw a lawful protest.
4. **Give factual testimony** if the jury calls you as a witness.
5. **Respect** the jury outcome after the process — no public “they got it wrong”.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **Chapter 11** plus links to **9.6** / **9.8** depending on the dispute subject.

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU may set its own filing windows — must be **published** in match regs. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **RO “settles”** a protest to avoid paperwork.
- **Destroying evidence** before the jury convenes.
- **Threatening** a competitor for using protest rights.
