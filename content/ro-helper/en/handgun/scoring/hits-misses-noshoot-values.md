## What this is

**9.4** sets the **score value** (or equivalent in your scoring system) for **hits**, **misses**, and hits on **no-shoots**, plus logic for relevant target types. Exact numbers, rounding, and interaction with **steel** are in the **Jan 2026** PDF and **target appendices**; the **RO** identifies hole type / steel response while **scoring** often applies the match standard.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Score paper** per **9.5** (zones, touches, overlaps) after an allowed approach — **9.4** gives meaning to the zones.
2. **Log steel:** knockdown / miss per stage rules and **Chapter 9**.
3. **No-shoots:** each no-shoot hit has a consequence under **9.4** — do not omit it on the sheet.
4. **Do not invent point values** “like last match” — only the current PDF and **appendices**.
5. **If paper is doubtful** — **CRO/RM** or a **9.6** challenge path, not a casual re-score.

### 9.4.1–9.4.2 — point table (Major/Minor)

Verify the numbers in your discipline PDF; for **Jan 2026** (IPSC) the typical table is:

- **9.4** works with **9.5** (paper) and **9.9** (moving / special targets if relevant).

## Common mistakes

- **Ignoring no-shoot** hits in the string total.
- **Mixing miss and FTE** — different rules (**9.5.6** etc. for FTE).
- **Assigning zones without tools** when the match requires a gauge.
