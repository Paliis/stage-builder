## What this is

**9.5.4** explains how to distinguish a **true bullet hole** from a **radial paper tear** and how to treat **enlarged** or irregular holes. This is critical for fair **zone** (A/C/D) assignment and to prevent abuse. Specific criteria (calibre overlay, outer-ring rules, etc.) are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF and **MD** policy.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Apply** the approved **gauge / overlay** when the match requires it for doubtful holes.
2. **Do not stretch** the hole edge to a more favourable zone without **9.5.4** support.
3. **Photograph** or retain the target per **MD** if a challenge is likely.
4. **Call CRO/RM** for systematically disputed targets on a stage.
5. **Brief the competitor** briefly **why** the hole was scored as scored (no long line debate).

## Common mistakes

- **Ignoring an obvious tear** beyond the projectile cut.
- **Different standards** across stages at the same match.
- **No record** — cannot review after teardown.
