## What this is

**9.7** defines how **scoresheets** (paper or electronic equivalents) are completed, **who signs**, how **corrections** are made after an error, and how this ties to **9.6** (challenges). The **RO** is responsible for a **correct primary entry** and for **not altering** records outside procedure.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Fill all mandatory fields:** stage, division, name, time, hits, procedurals, RO signature as **MD** requires.
2. **Sign** only after agreement with the competitor where the rules require a signature.
3. **Corrections:** only per **9.7** (strike-through, initials, **RM**/scoring confirmation — confirm in PDF and briefing).
4. **After a protest** — do not change the sheet alone; follow the authorised decision.
5. **Copies / photos** if **MD** requires them for archive or online systems.

## Common mistakes

- **Correction without initials** — cannot audit the change.
- **Picking the “better” of two times** without a rules basis.
- **Erasing** instead of the required strike-and-sign pattern.
