## What this is

**Appendix C1** of **Handgun Jan 2026** describes the **popper calibration challenge**: when a competitor disputes whether a **popper** fell because of **correct calibration** (energy / mechanical behaviour), what **evidence** is required, **who** decides, and how the **RM** runs any **test shot** or equivalent procedure. Exact steps, fees, and time windows are **only** in **C1** — do not copy procedures from older editions or other disciplines’ appendices.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop scoring arguments** on the bay; record **facts** (popper state, shots fired, timer) and direct the competitor to the **challenge path** in **C1**.
2. **Preserve the stage** as required (do not reset targets) until **RM** / **CRO** says otherwise.
3. **Do not promise** a favourable outcome or run an unofficial “test pop” without **RM** authorisation.
4. **Give a neutral statement** if **RM** interviews you about what you saw.
5. **Know** that **PCC / Rifle / Mini Rifle / Shotgun** may use a **different appendix letter** for the same idea — always open the PDF for the match discipline.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)


### RO algorithm: popper calibration challenge

- **Do not touch the popper before calibration.** No adjustments, no “hand tests”.
- Record **facts** (string, target state, conditions), stop arguments on the line, and escalate to **RM/CRO** per **Appendix C1**.
- Use the **calibration gun** / calibration shot procedure per **Appendix C1**.
- If the popper **falls** with the calibration shot — the competitor’s hits on it score **0** (i.e. **Miss**).
- If the popper **does not fall** with the calibration shot — a **Reshoot** is mandatory (range equipment failure; see **4.6.1**).

- Anchor: **Appendix C1** (Handgun); cross-link to **Chapter 9** (scoring) and **Chapter 4** (range equipment) where the PDF ties them.

## Common mistakes

- **Tuning the popper** yourself after a complaint instead of escalating to **RM**.
- **Destroying evidence** (moving steel, erasing scores) before review.
- **Treating** a handgun **C1** procedure as identical to another discipline’s appendix without reading that PDF.
