## What this is

**9.9** governs scoring for **moving** and **disappearing** targets (and related mechanisms). In **Handgun Jan 2026**, read sub-clauses carefully: e.g. **9.9.2** separates **moving** target cases from **disappearing** cases; also check **activator** / target-chain logic in the **WSB** and PDF. Outcomes for misses, re-presentation, unactivated chains are **only** from the official text.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read the WSB:** activation order, timings, second shot requirements, **reset** behaviour.
2. **Observe** target response (movement, drop, disappear) from an allowed position; use a second official or video per **MD** policy on complex stages.
3. **Scoresheet** per **9.9** (do not import rules from another discipline or an old edition).
4. **Mechanical failure** — **RM** before more runs on that stage.
5. **Brief the competitor** on observed facts only; disputes — **9.6**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)


### 9.9.2–9.9.3 — disappearing vs moving, and equipment malfunction

- **Disappearing target**: if a target is legally defined as “disappearing”, **not engaging / not hitting** it does **not** incur **Miss/FTE** (see **9.9.2–9.9.3** logic).
- If the mechanism **malfunctions** and the target **fails to disappear** (remains partially visible / hangs up), treat it as **range equipment failure** → **Reshoot per 4.6.1**.
- Note: “moving but still visible” ≠ “disappearing”. Classify via **WSB** + **9.9.2–9.9.3**.

- Read **9.9** end-to-end; matrix note: split **9.9.2** (moving vs disappearing) and **activator** logic.

## Common mistakes

- **Scoring like a static target** when **9.9** applies.
- **Ignoring** that the chain was never activated per **WSB**.
- **Continuing to shoot** on broken gear without **RM** direction.
