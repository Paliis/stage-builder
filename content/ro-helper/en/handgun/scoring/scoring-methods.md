## What this is

**9.2** **IPSC Handgun** defines **scoring methods** (time dependence, bonuses, caps, etc. — **exact names and formulas in the Jan 2026** PDF). The bay **RO** usually does **not** calculate match points mentally: the job is to **record** time, hits, penalties, and the **WSB** stage type correctly so **scoring** can apply the right method from **9.2**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Confirm the WSB:** which **scoring method** applies to this stage (and that it matches the match schedule).
2. **Complete the scoresheet** data set: time, A/C/D or equivalent, procedurals, extra fields required by **MD** / software.
3. **Do not swap methods** ad hoc if the stage was registered differently — only via **RM/MD**.
4. **Timer failures** — reshoot or correction per match rules, not an improvised formula.
5. **Explain to the competitor** only what concerns the **record** (do not debate Comstock math on the bay).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

## Local (FPSU)

Even single-method matches must brief consistently with the rules. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Mixing methods** across stages on the same day.
- **Incomplete scoresheet** — scoring cannot apply **9.2** correctly.
- **RO “fixes” time** without a rules basis.
