## What this is

**9.3** explains how to **break ties** when aggregate results match (or match after software rounding). This is a **match-level** decision (**chief scoring / MD / RM** per regs), not a bay **RO** call. The **RO** supplies **clean scoresheet inputs** so a tie can be resolved correctly at all.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Do not declare winners** for a stage or division on the line when tied — only confirm **COF** completion and target review.
2. **Avoid verbal rounding**; log what the **WSB** and software require (timer time, hit counts, etc.).
3. **If a local tie appears** from your sheet (same time and same points after your entry) — hand the package to **scoring**; do not invent a shoot-off unless **9.3** and the **MD** allow it.
4. **Keep the scoresheet** free of “cosmetic” edits so **9.3** applies to authentic data.
5. **Point competitors** to official results / tablets — do not argue placements.

## Common mistakes

- **RO ranks competitors** without scoring — outside authority.
- **Post-signature sheet edits** “to break a tie” — data integrity breach.
- **Confusing with protests** (**9.6**) — different process.
