## What this is

**9.6** governs **verification** of recorded results and **challenges** (protests) to scoring decisions or related issues. It sets **time limits**, **who** may file, **what evidence** counts, and how cases escalate to **RM** / arbitration. The line **RO** is often the **first contact**: do **not** block a lawful challenge and do **not** promise outcomes outside your authority.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Explain the process** to the competitor: where to go, by when, in what form (oral/written — per **MD** and **9.6**).
2. **Preserve evidence:** targets, photos, timer, scoresheet — per **RM** instructions; do not tear down before allowed.
3. **Do not alter a signed sheet** alone after a challenge — only per authorised decision.
4. **Give a neutral fact statement** if **RM** interviews the RO.
5. **Do not engage** in aggressive debate — call **CRO/RM**.

## Common mistakes

- **Refusing a challenge** for “no time” when the PDF grants the right.
- **Destroying targets** before review.
- **RO changes the score** under pressure without **RM**.
