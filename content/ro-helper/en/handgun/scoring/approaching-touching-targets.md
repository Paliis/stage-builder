## What this is

**9.1** **IPSC Handgun** governs **when** a competitor may **approach** targets (paper, steel, etc.) and what counts as a forbidden **touch** or premature approach. This affects **scoring integrity** and sometimes **procedural** penalties — exact wording is **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Control the firing line / end of COF:** allow approach only after commands required by the rules and briefing (e.g. **“If clear, hammer down”** — confirm in **Chapters 8** and **9**).
2. **Stop** the competitor verbally if they move to a target **too early** under the rules.
3. **Log breaches** for scoring: time, target, whether there was contact / movement of the target.
4. **Do not allow “fixes”** to paper or poppers without rule permission — risks voided results.
5. **Notify scoring / RM** if the incident may change the score or trigger **DQ** (if the PDF links that).

## Common mistakes

- **RO looks away** after the last shot — misses a premature approach.
- **Allowing “just checking the hole”** — may breach **9.1**.
- **No log** — dispute over whether a target was moved.
