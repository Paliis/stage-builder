## What this is

On IPSC **paper** targets, **A, C, D** zones (exact boundaries on the target artwork in **appendices**) carry different **weight** in scoring. **Major** vs **Minor** for the division / match changes **multipliers** and the “price” of misses and no-shoots — all tabulated with **Chapter 9** in the **Jan 2026** PDF. The **RO** must **assign the correct zone** per hole; **scoring** applies Major/Minor from the competitor’s registration.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Confirm** the **correct target type** (mini, classic, etc.) per **WSB**.
2. **Assign the hit zone** under **9.5** (boundary lines — per PDF, not by eye).
3. **Enter letters/zones** on the scoresheet as the match software expects (A/C/D or equivalent).
4. **Do not swap Major/Minor** on the bay — that is **registration** / division data; errors go to **scoring / RM**.
5. **Borderline holes** — overlay / **CRO** per match policy.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **9.5** plus tables at the end of the rules / appendices; align with **9.4** (hit values).

## Local (FPSU)

If the match is announced as **Minor** for everyone, still verify against **MD**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Wrong target type** (mini vs full) — different zones.
- **“Friendly” zone calls** without a rule basis.
- **Incomplete entries** — scoring cannot apply Major/Minor correctly.
