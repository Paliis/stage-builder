## What this is

**9.5** is the core section for IPSC **paper** targets: how to assign **zones** (A, C, D, etc.), how to treat **lines** between zones, **no-shoot** touches, **radial tears**, **FTE**, combinations with **steel**, etc. This is the most common source of bay arguments; answers are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF and the match **target appendices**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Approach targets** only after allowed commands (**9.1**, **Chapter 8**).
2. **Use approved overlays / gauges** when **MD** or match rules require them for doubtful holes.
3. **Log each target** on the **scoresheet** or app as scoring requires (zones, hit counts, no-shoots).
4. **If still ambiguous** — **CRO** or **RM** procedure, not a spectator vote.
5. **Explain** the rule reference or show the measurement to the competitor; challenges per **9.6**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **9.5** with **9.4** (values) and **9.5.4** / other sub-rules on paper damage — situation dependent.
- Related cards in the pack: **C180** (Major/Minor zones), **C185** (radial tears), **C190** (FTE scoring side).

## Local (FPSU)

“One RO signs paper” vs “two officials” — per **MD**; fine if it improves accuracy without contradicting IPSC. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Eyeballing** instead of applying the PDF zone boundary.
- **Missing a no-shoot touch** that is barely visible.
- **Late changes** after signing the sheet without **9.6** / **RM** process.
