## What this is

**Significant advantage** means the competitor, during the **course of fire (COF)**, gains an **unfair advantage** compared with the intended stage design—typically by shooting from a position that materially improves target access beyond what the **WSB** / stage layout allows. In the **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules**, start from **10.2.1.1** and **10.2.2**; take the **exact** qualification and penalty count **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record:** the competitor’s actual position; what creates the advantage; whether it contradicts the **WSB** or general position limits.
2. **Compare to fair execution:** what lawful performance would look like; if unclear, involve **CRO** or **RM**.
3. **Apply the penalty:** per **10.2.1.1** / **10.2.2** after reading **Chapter 10** in the PDF.
4. **Brief the competitor:** briefly which **advantage** was significant.
5. **Document:** WSB reference, area, shot linkage.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Key clauses for Handgun: **10.2.1.1**, **10.2.2** — read in context within **Chapter 10**.
- Do not default to generic **10.1** when the rulebook routes the case to the **advantage** clauses.

## Common mistakes

- **Purely subjective calls** without an auditable breach of the WSB/stage facts.
- **Double punishment** for the same act under two rules without PDF support.
- **Scoresheet notes** with no shot / COF element reference.
