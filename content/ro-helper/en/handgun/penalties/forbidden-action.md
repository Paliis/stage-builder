## What this is

**10.2.11** in **IPSC Handgun** covers situations where the competitor **must not** shoot the way they did — including (per PDF wording) shots **over** or **through** certain types of cover / barriers (the matrix label says “tall barrier” — **do not** replace reading the rule with the label). Exact geometry, exceptions, and **procedural** count (or other outcome) are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF and from the **WSB** if the stage clarifies allowed windows.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record:** trajectory / line of fire relative to the barrier; whether a shot occurred in an area the PDF forbids for that structure type.
2. **Check the WSB:** for local clarification (sometimes the stage defines an allowed sector differently from a “general” rule — **only if compatible with the rulebook**).
3. **Apply the sanction** strictly per **10.2.11** after PDF verification; do not swap with **10.2.5** (Cooper tunnel) if the facts fit a different clause — read both if the boundary is unclear.
4. **Scoresheet** entry tied to shot / position / stage element.
5. **Escalate to CRO/RM** if stage props are ambiguous against the rule text.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.2.11** in **Chapter 10**; read the full paragraph and any cross-references in that chapter.
- If there is an **unsafe** angle risk — safety procedures first, then scoring.

## Common mistakes

- **Mixing with Cooper tunnel (10.2.5):** different sub-rules — different conditions in the PDF.
- **Guessing the penalty:** without mapping to **10.2.11**, the entry is hard to defend.
- **Bad RO angle:** if you cannot see the line of fire, involve a second official or **RM** per match policy.
