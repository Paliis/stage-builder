## What this is

**Failure to engage (FTE)** on this card means the competitor **does not** deliver the required shot/hit on a target that the **course of fire (COF)** or **Written Stage Briefing (WSB)** mandates, when a **penalty** path is governed under **Chapter 10** (**10.2.7** — confirm exact wording in the **Jan 2026** **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules** PDF). **Scoring** consequences for misses / non-engaged targets often sit under **9.5.6** — check **Chapter 9** and **Chapter 10** **together** against the primary source.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record:** which target had to be engaged; whether a shot occurred; whether you have a clear **miss** or **skip** relative to the COF.
2. **Check the WSB:** confirm the target was **mandatory** for that element; rule out a different penalty clause if it fits better.
3. **Apply scoring and penalty:** per **9.5.6** and **10.2.7** after reading the tables and text in the official PDF.
4. **Brief the competitor:** briefly which COF element and which target (no debate on the line).
5. **Document:** shot number / target label / WSB tie-in on the **scoresheet**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)


### 10.2.7 + 9.5.6 — FTE: per target + misses

- **FTE (Failure To Engage)**: penalty logic is **one procedural per target** under **10.2.7**.
- In addition, apply **Misses** for the un-hit required shots (scoring mechanics per **9.5.6** and your PDF tables).
- Net: **procedural per target + misses**, not “misses only” and not “procedural per shot”.

- **10.2.7** — primary **penalty** anchor for FTE-style faults in Handgun context (details in PDF).
- **9.5.6** — primary **scoring** anchor; do not treat scoring and penalties as disconnected if the rulebook links them.

## Common mistakes

- **Scoring-only or penalty-only:** always check whether **9.x** and **10.x** must both be applied for the same fact pattern.
- **Wrong target:** verify the target the WSB/COF actually required to be shot.
- **Vague scoresheet:** “FTE” with no target ID / shot number.
