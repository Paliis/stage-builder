## What this is

**Cooper tunnel** in IPSC slang describes a pattern where the competitor **“covers”** a port (approach from above / specific geometry relative to a window or tunnel), and **Handgun** **10.2.5** states **when** such behaviour with a shot incurs **procedural** (or other outcome). Exact criteria and exceptions are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF — do not confuse with **10.2.11** if the rulebook separates the cases.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Observe:** how the competitor enters the window / tunnel; body and handgun position; whether a shot occurs in a disputed posture.
2. **Verify against the PDF:** whether **10.2.5** conditions are met (do not substitute your own “covered” definition).
3. **Apply the penalty** per **10.2.5**; **scoresheet** entry with a short episode description.
4. **If stage props are ambiguous** (window height, shelf, etc.) — **RM** per match procedure.
5. **Safety first** if muzzle direction is unsafe alongside the cover issue.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.2.5** in **Chapter 10**; cross-check **10.2.11** if it looks like a forbidden shot over/through cover.

## Common mistakes

- **Label without rule:** “tunnel” without checking **10.2.5** text.
- **Bad RO sightline:** cannot confirm overhead cover — second official or video per policy.
- **Confusing with WSB position:** a WSB-allowed approach must still be **compatible** with the rulebook — not “WSB vs PDF”.
