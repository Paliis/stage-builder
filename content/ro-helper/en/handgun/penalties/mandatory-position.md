## What this is

The **WSB** may require shooting from a **specific position** (standing / kneeling / through a port, etc.), from a **defined area**, or with the **strong / weak hand**. If the competitor **does not** comply during the **COF**, **Handgun** rules normally tie this to **10.2.2** (position / zone / “significant advantage” per PDF wording) and, when the issue is **which hand**, to **10.2.8**. Definitions and **procedural** counts are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF — do not merge sub-clauses without reading the text.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Extract from the WSB:** what is **mandatory** (position, zone, hand, order of moves).
2. **Observe facts:** from which position / zone shots were fired; whether hand matched WSB if regulated.
3. **Pick the clause:** if the failure is **hand** per WSB, check whether **10.2.8** is primary; if **zone/position**, check **10.2.2** (and whether **10.2.1** fits better for a foot fault).
4. **Scoresheet** entry per the chosen sub-rule after PDF verification.
5. **Brief the competitor** briefly; involve **CRO/RM** if WSB interpretation is disputed.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.2.2** and **10.2.8** live in **Chapter 10** of the Handgun PDF; read full paragraphs for hierarchy and exceptions, not only matrix labels.
- If the failure is actually a **foot fault**, **10.2.1** may apply instead of WSB position.

## Common mistakes

- **One penalty for everything:** the WSB may stack requirements — the PDF may treat situations differently.
- **Strong/weak hand when WSB is silent:** **10.2.8** may not apply if the WSB does not fix the hand.
- **Split RO team:** align ambiguous WSB lines with **RM** before start when possible.
