## What this is

A **mandatory reload** in the **WSB** means the competitor **must** perform a reload (type, point in the **COF**, count, etc.) exactly as written. If the competitor **fails** to meet that requirement (skip, wrong order, substituting shots without the required reload, etc.), the penalty anchor is **10.2.4** in **IPSC Handgun** — exact conditions and **procedural** count **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read the WSB:** where the reload is **mandatory**; single episode or several; any stated exceptions (e.g. after a malfunction).
2. **Record facts:** whether a reload occurred; whether it matches the requirement (e.g. tactical vs administrative — **per WSB and rules text only**).
3. **Apply the penalty:** scoresheet entry per **10.2.4** after PDF check — do not mis-bucket under other **10.2** clauses (e.g. **10.2.2** if the issue is reload, not position).
4. **Brief the competitor:** briefly which WSB requirement was missed (challenges per **9.6**).
5. **Document:** WSB clause + moment in **COF** so the entry is auditable.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Primary anchor: **10.2.4** (details **only** from the official **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules** PDF, **Chapter 10**).
- If another clause fits better (e.g. generic **10.1** or **10.2.2**) — use **that** clause after reading the rulebook.

## Common mistakes

- **Confusing with free order:** if the WSB does **not** mandate a reload at that point, **10.2.4** may not apply.
- **Vague scoresheet:** “no reload” without the WSB line — weak under protest.
- **Wrong bucket:** do not force **10.2.4** when the PDF supports a different rule.
