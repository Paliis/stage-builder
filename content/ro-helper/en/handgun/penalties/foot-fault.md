## What this is

A **foot fault** is when the competitor, during the **course of fire (COF)**, touches the ground **outside** the allowed area or crosses a **fault line** in a way forbidden by the **Written Stage Briefing (WSB)** or the general rules for lines on the stage. In the **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules** this is normally anchored under **10.2.1** (see **10.2.1.1** in your **Jan 2026** PDF).

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record:** which **fault line** / area applies; whether foot/shoe contact occurred; when during the **COF** (before/during a shot, while moving, etc.).
2. **Check the WSB:** whether the line is **active** for that position; avoid mis-labelling (e.g. generic **10.1** if **10.2.1** clearly applies).
3. **Apply the penalty:** enter on the **scoresheet** per **10.2.1** / **10.2.1.1** after verification against the PDF — do not guess procedural counts.
4. **Brief the competitor:** briefly what triggered the penalty (no debate on the line; challenges per **9.6**).
5. **Document:** tie the entry to shot / side / WSB condition so it is auditable.

Under **FPSU / UPSF** rules, numbering and zone names may differ; verify against the official FPSU text. **`fpsu_delta_verified`** stays **false** until reviewer sign-off per **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1**.

## Common mistakes

- **Wrong bucket:** foot faults sit under **10.2.1** — do not default to **10.1** when the rulebook assigns **10.2.1**.
- **Bad angle:** the RO cannot see contact — involve **CRO** or **RM** and match video/policy if available.
- **Vague scoresheet:** “foot fault” with no shot / line side — weak for protests.
