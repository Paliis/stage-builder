## What this is

**Creeping** is prohibited **movement** or body/gun **lean** **before** the start command (**“Start”** or equivalent) that gives the competitor an advantage in readiness for the first shot. In the **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules**, this is normally addressed under **10.2.6** (confirm full text and exceptions in the **Jan 2026** PDF).

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Observe** the start line / ready area and the **Stand by** / **Start** sequence per match SOP.
2. **Record:** foot movement, body shift, or gun movement before the signal; any extra limits in the **WSB**.
3. **Apply the penalty:** per **10.2.6** after PDF check; if unclear, **Chief Range Officer (CRO)** or **Range Master (RM)**.
4. **Brief the competitor:** briefly what movement was seen.
5. **Document:** “creeping / 10.2.6”, COF stage reference, timing relative to commands.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.2.6** — **Chapter 10** of the official **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules** (Jan 2026).
- Do not conflate with other pre-start issues (e.g. **Make ready** / **Are you ready** procedures — read the rulebook and WSB).

## Common mistakes

- **Micro-movement vs genuine creeping:** apply the **PDF** test, not vibes alone.
- **Inconsistent calls between bays:** align with **CRO**.
- **No note of which command** was given — weak evidence in a protest.
