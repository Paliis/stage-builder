## What this is

A **dropped firearm** (the handgun **falls** from the hand / holster during the **COF** or in another moment covered by the rule) under **IPSC Handgun** is normally classified under **10.5.3** and leads to **disqualification (DQ)**. Whether the rule covers drops **from the holster**, during **reload**, inside the **Safety Area**, etc. is **only** in the **Jan 2026** PDF — do not substitute “common sense” for the text.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Safety:** do not try to catch a falling gun; step behind the safety line; **“STOP”** / **“CEASE FIRE”** per protocol.
2. **Control the area:** nobody approaches the pistol until **RM** / designated staff command.
3. **Record:** what fell (complete gun vs magazine — magazines may fall under **other** rules); from which action (movement, reload, holster); whether a shot occurred.
4. **Escalate to RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.3** if regs require confirmation.
5. **Document** for the DQ paperwork.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.5.3** in **Section 10.5**; cross-check **10.4** if a shot happened during the drop.

## Common mistakes

- **DQ for a magazine:** if the PDF treats magazines separately — do not force **10.5.3** without basis.
- **Competitor picks up the gun** before command — extra safety risk and procedure breach.
- **No witnesses / description** — hard to defend the decision under protest.
