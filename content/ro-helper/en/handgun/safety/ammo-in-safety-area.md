## What this is

**10.5.14** in **Handgun Jan 2026** addresses **ammunition** (or related items per PDF wording) in the **Safety Area** — usually **DQ** because it raises **AD** risk and breaks zone isolation. What counts as “ammunition”, empty-case exceptions, etc. are **only** in the official text — do not import myths from other rule editions.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** that live rounds do not appear on belts / tables in the **Safety Area** during gun handling.
2. **Stop** the breach verbally; if the competitor refuses to clear it — **RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.14** after confirmation.
3. **Do not grab** someone else’s ammo — direct the owner; if unsafe — **CEASE FIRE** per protocol.
4. **Brief newcomers** on where the **Safety Area** boundary is.
5. **Do not confuse** with allowed **empty magazine** rules if the PDF separates them.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.5.14**; read with **Chapter 2** (Safety Area) and **10.3** (DQ administration).

## Local (FPSU)

**Safety Area** markings must be visible; extra bans — **MD**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **“Just one round”** in a pocket in the Safety Area when the PDF forbids it.
- **RO carries** ammo while entering the gun-service zone.
- **Ignoring** a breach to avoid awkwardness.
