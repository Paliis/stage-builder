## What this is

An **accidental discharge (AD)** under **IPSC Handgun** is a shot fired **outside** controlled fire on the **RO’s command** during the **COF**, or in other situations **explicitly listed in Section 10.4** of the **Jan 2026** PDF. The outcome is usually **disqualification (DQ)**; any **exceptions** (e.g. regarding the **Safety Area**) are **only** from the official text — not from memory.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Safety first:** **“CEASE FIRE”** / **“STOP”** per match protocol; muzzle control and distance to the competitor.
2. **Freeze gun handling** after the stop until **RM** / designated staff arrive if the briefing requires it.
3. **Record facts:** time, place on the range, trigger state, RO command before the shot, witnesses.
4. **Do not declare DQ alone** if match regs require **RM** confirmation — follow procedure; after decision, process **DQ** per **10.4**.
5. **Hand off to RM** for paperwork and follow-up (media, stats, etc. — per match policy).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)


### 10.4.2 — shot into the ground (distance)

- **10.4.2**: a shot **into the ground** within **3 meters** (3 m) — **DQ** per the rule.

- Anchor: **10.4**; read all sub-clauses and cross-references (e.g. minimum distance to steel if relevant).
- Do not confuse AD with **malfunctions** or **reshoots** — other sections.

## Common mistakes

- **“Almost AD” without 10.4 text:** wrong DQ or no DQ.
- **Panic over protocol:** loud commands without line control and witnesses.
- **No written record:** incident cannot be reconstructed.
