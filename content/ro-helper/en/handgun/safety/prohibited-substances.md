## What this is

**10.7** **IPSC Handgun** prohibits being under the influence of listed **substances** (typically alcohol and drugs per the PDF) on the range and/or while competing — exact wording and consequences (**usually DQ**) are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF. This targets **competitors** and may extend to other persons if the rule says so.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Do not solo-investigate:** if there is suspicion (odour, behaviour, report) — **RM/MD** per match procedure.
2. **Keep the range safe:** if there is **clear** impairment risk, stop the **COF** per protocol.
3. **Record objectively:** what was observed (no medical diagnoses from the RO); time; place.
4. **Hand off to RM** for a **10.7** decision and **DQ** paperwork if the basis is confirmed under match rules and the PDF.
5. **Respect privacy and law:** testing / law-enforcement steps only within regs and legal limits.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.7**; read with **10.3** on how DQ is declared.
- Do not merge with **10.6** unsportsmanlike conduct if the PDF separates the grounds.

## Local (FPSU)

National law and MD policy may add requirements (e.g. alcohol bans before squads); verify. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Public accusation** without **RM** escalation — reputation and procedure risk.
- **DQ on hearsay:** rumours are insufficient; follow the PDF and match process.
- **Ignoring obvious impairment** to avoid conflict.
