## What this is

**10.5.1** frames **unsafe firearm handling** that is **not** covered by a **more specific** sub-rule in **Section 10.5** or **10.4**. If the facts clearly fit **finger in guard**, **180°**, **dropped gun**, **AD**, etc. — apply **that** rule instead of a vague **10.5.1** unless the PDF allows it. Outcome is usually **DQ** — verify in the **Jan 2026** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop the hazard:** commands per protocol; muzzle control; distance.
2. **Classify:** first check for a **more specific** rule (**10.4**, **10.5.2**, **10.5.3**, **10.5.9–11**, etc.).
3. **If only general 10.5.1 remains** — document the concrete unsafe act and escalate to **RM** if required.
4. **Do not invent DQ** without rule text support.
5. **Short log** for paperwork: act + **COF** phase + witnesses.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Treat **10.5.1** as a **residual** bucket — read the **10.5** intro and neighbouring sub-rules to avoid double or wrong qualification.

## Local (FPSU)

Match regs may add local bans; they must not contradict IPSC **DQ** bases from the PDF. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Everything into 10.5.1:** “didn’t like the handling” with no sub-rule — weak under protest.
- **Missing the specific rule:** e.g. factual **AD** should be **10.4**, not a generic clause.
- **Delaying the stop** for doubt — safety beats finishing the string.
