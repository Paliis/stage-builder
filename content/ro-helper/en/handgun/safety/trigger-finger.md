## What this is

**Chapter 10.5** **Handgun** requires the **finger outside the trigger guard** in defined **COF** phases (movement, reload, not on target, etc.). Sub-rules **10.5.9–10.5.11** in **Jan 2026** split the situations; the outcome is usually **DQ**, but **which** sub-rule and any exceptions are **PDF-only**. The card matrix cites **10.5.9–10.5.11** — tighten quotes in `ipsc_refs.note` during human review.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch the finger** from an allowed position; if unsure — second official or **CRO**, not a guess.
2. **Map the COF phase:** moving between positions, reloading, aiming on target — different paragraphs in **10.5.9–10.5.11**.
3. **For DQ-level breaches** — stop the **COF** per safety protocol; call **RM** if regs require confirmation.
4. **Document:** what the competitor was doing (hands, target, RO command) before the finger entered the guard.
5. **Do not substitute “friendly warnings”** for applying the rule — that undermines fairness.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **10.5.9**, **10.5.10**, **10.5.11** in full; do not merge with **10.5.2** (180°) or **10.4** (AD) if the fact pattern differs.

## Local (FPSU)

Clubs stress “index finger high”; official outcome follows match rules. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **DQ without a phase:** “finger in guard” with no tie to **10.5.9/10/11** — weak under protest.
- **Bad RO angle:** if the guard was not visible, do not invent a violation; bring another official.
- **Ignoring start/stop:** behaviour before/after commands may fall under other **10.5** sub-rules.
