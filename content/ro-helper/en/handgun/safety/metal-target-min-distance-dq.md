## What this is

**10.4.7** in **Handgun Jan 2026** covers **disqualification** when a competitor engages **steel** from closer than the distance set in the PDF (and any combined conditions — **read the full sub-clause**). This is a **critical** safety rule; **ROs** must know published match minimums and **not allow** a stage layout that forces a breach.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Check WSB and layout:** distances to poppers / plates vs **10.4.7** and **MD** briefing.
2. **Stop the COF** if mid-string it becomes clear the competitor **violated** minimum distance — then **RM** for **DQ** under **10.4.7**.
3. **Do not “measure by eye”** for the official call — involve **RM** / tools if the match defines a method.
4. **Warn MD** before the match if a stage is **impossible** without breaking **10.4.7**.
5. **Document** for the DQ record.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.4.7** inside **10.4**; do not merge with other **10.4** items (AD, etc.).

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU may use **stricter** minimums; apply the **stricter** match rule. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Publishing** a stage that geometrically forces a shot inside the PDF limit.
- **RO unaware** of the official metre line on the bay.
- **Warning instead of DQ** when the PDF requires **10.4.7**.
