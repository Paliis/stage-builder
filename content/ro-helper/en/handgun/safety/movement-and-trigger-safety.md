## What this is

**8.5** in **IPSC Handgun** describes how to **move** with the firearm during the **COF** (body, speed, muzzle control — **PDF details**). It does **not** fully duplicate **10.5.9–10.5.11** on **finger in the trigger guard** (card **C115**), but in practice the RO checks **both**: safe movement under **8.5**, then whether a separate **DQ** finger breach exists under **10.5.9–10.5.11**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Keep distance and angle** to see **both** muzzle and finger while the competitor moves.
2. **Command** per protocol when unsafe (**“STOP”**, etc.) — do not wait for the string to finish.
3. **Classify:** if the issue is the **finger** — **C115** / **10.5.9–11**; if it is **how** the handgun is carried while moving — **8.5** (outcome in the PDF may differ from DQ — verify).
4. **Document** for **RM** if the sanction or interpretation is unclear.
5. **Pre-brief** common movement pitfalls if the **MD** assigns it.

## Common mistakes

- **Everything labelled “finger”:** ignoring **8.5** when the PDF separates movement discipline from finger rules.
- **RO too close** — cannot see muzzle on lateral movement.
- **Warnings instead of STOP** on clear danger.
