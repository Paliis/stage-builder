## What this is

**10.6** **IPSC Handgun** defines **unsportsmanlike** behaviour (insults, cheating, undermining officials, score manipulation, etc. — **exact list in the PDF**). Outcomes are usually severe (**disqualification** or other sanctions per sub-rule) and require an **official** **RM/MD** decision, not ad-hoc arguments on the bay.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Do not solo-escalate:** record **facts** (quote, act, time, witnesses); leave **10.6** classification to **RM**.
2. **Stay calm and professional:** do not trade insults; do not threaten instant DQ if procedure says otherwise.
3. **Keep the match safe:** if behaviour disrupts others’ **COF** — temporarily remove the competitor per **CRO/RM** command.
4. **Refer to MD** in writing/oral form per match briefing.
5. **Do not merge** with **8.6** (assistance) or **10.7** (substances) if the PDF separates grounds.

## Common mistakes

- **RO punishes alone:** without **RM** — procedural breach and protest risk.
- **Vague labels:** “unpleasant person” with no fact tied to **10.6**.
- **Public shouting match** instead of documented escalation up the chain.
