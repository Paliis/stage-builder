## What this is

**Chapter 2** of **IPSC Handgun** defines baseline **range** layout: **Safety Area**, gun handling off the firing line, movement between zones, etc. Breaches here often yield **DQ** or other sanctions **before** the **COF** starts; exact zone boundaries and prohibitions are **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF (sub-clause numbering must be confirmed during review).

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Know the match map:** where the **Safety Area** is, firing lines, hot/cold areas per **MD** briefing.
2. **Stop breaches immediately** (gun outside allowed areas, ammunition where forbidden — per **Chapter 2** and **Chapter 10** text).
3. **Do not grant “exceptions”** unless rules and **RM** allow them.
4. **Escalate** borderline cases (boundary crossings, temporary layout changes) to **RM**.
5. **Brief newcomers** briefly where guns and ammo may go — prevention beats DQ.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **Chapter 2** as a whole; cross-check **10.5**, **10.4**, **10.5.14** (if relevant to ammunition in the **Safety Area** in your edition).

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU range layouts may differ; follow local diagrams and **CRO** directions. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Different standards for different competitors:** erodes match trust.
- **RO does not know Safety Area limits** — cannot explain a violation.
- **Ignoring “small” magazine carries** in prohibited zones.
