## What this is

The â€ś180-degree ruleâ€ť is a fundamental IPSC safety principle. It requires the muzzle to stay **downrange** within the **designated safe sector**. If the muzzle crosses the imaginary line parallel to the backstop (or boundaries specified in the **Written Stage Briefing (WSB)**), that is a **critical safety violation**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop:** Immediately issue a loud, clear **â€śSTOP!â€ť** command.
2. **Control:** Approach the competitor (only if safe), ensure they cease fire, and hold the firearm in a safe direction.
3. **Unload:** Direct the competitor to unload and show clear per match procedures.
4. **Holster / dismissal:** After safety is verified, order the firearm holstered. Under **10.5.2**, a muzzle-safe-angle breach is normally a **match disqualification (DQ)** â€” confirm exact wording in the official **IPSC Handgun Competition Rules** PDF for your edition (this card uses **Jan 2026**), **Chapter 10**. The competitor does not continue the match after a DQ under this rule.
5. **Report:** Summon the **Range Master (RM)**. Record **â€śDQ â€” Rule 10.5.2 (180Â° / muzzle safe angles)â€ť** on the **scoresheet**, with time and location of the violation.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Per **10.5.2**, disqualification applies if, at any time during the **course of fire (COF)**, the competitor allows the muzzle to point rearwards (**further than 90Â°** from the median perpendicular line to the backstop), or outside **muzzle safe angles** specified in the **Written Stage Briefing (WSB)**.

- This applies during **movement**, **malfunction clearance**, and **reloading**.
