## What this is

Pointing the **muzzle** at a **person** (spectator, official, another competitor, staff, etc.) during the **course of fire** is a critical range-safety breach. The RO must respond **immediately** and unambiguously.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **STOP!**—cease fire and movement; establish a safe **muzzle** direction.
2. **Do not “grade severity” ad hoc:** if the facts meet **10.5.6** (or the discipline equivalent), process as **DQ** after verifying the PDF.
3. **Unload / clear the line** per match protocol; after a DQ the competitor does **not** continue the match.
4. **RM + documentation:** time, place on stage, who was in the sector, what the RO saw (angle, distance, COF phase).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

On **Handgun** Jan 2026, **10.5.6** covers pointing the **muzzle** at **any person** (including another competitor) during the COF—normally **DQ**. Confirm exact text and any carve-outs in the official PDF.

- Distinguish from **sweeping across the competitor’s own body** (**10.5.5**) and from **180°** (**10.5.2**) when those anchors fit better.

## Common mistakes

- Writing “pointed at someone” without tying it to **COF phase** and observable **muzzle** orientation.
- Defaulting to **10.5.1** when **10.5.6** clearly applies.
