## What this is

**Section 10.3** of **IPSC Handgun** collects **general** rules on **disqualification (DQ)**: when it applies, how it is declared, its effect on further participation, etc. **Specific** DQ grounds (AD, 180°, dropped gun, finger in guard, etc.) sit in **10.4**, **10.5**, **10.7**, etc. — **10.3** helps **apply** them administratively.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Confirm a basis in the rulebook** (usually **Chapter 10**, or elsewhere if the PDF says so), not a gut call.
2. **Stop the COF** and keep the range safe until the process is complete.
3. **Inform the competitor** per **10.3** and the match briefing (who declares, where, in what form).
4. **Hand RM/MD** the package for the official record: rule citation, facts, witnesses, time, match stage.
5. **Avoid long line debates** — challenges per **9.6** where applicable.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **10.3** together with the sub-rule that supplies the **DQ ground**.
- Check the PDF for whether DQ **ends** all further match participation or any nuances for teams / multiple disciplines at the same event.

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU may require extra paperwork or medical follow-up after incidents — follow the regs. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **DQ without a rule cite:** “unsafe” with no clause — unacceptable for the official record.
- **RO declares without authority:** if **10.3** or the MD requires **RM** — follow it.
- **Confusing with WD:** voluntary withdrawal — different rule logic.
