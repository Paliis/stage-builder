## What this is

For **handgun**, this card covers **holsters** and **carry** between stages and on the make-ready line: allowed types, **retention** level, belt position, cant, etc. — in **Chapter 5** and division **appendices** (**Production**, **Open**, etc.). Slings and **long-gun** carry are not the handgun focus; see other discipline rows in the matrix for PCC/rifle.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Inspect the holster** for division compliance (open / closed, retention, banned “speed” designs — **PDF**).
2. **Check the draw start:** does the holster compromise safety on the first move (**8.5**, **10.5**).
3. **Hold the start** on clear non-compliance; escalate to **RM** for a final call.
4. **Do not allow** improvised tape rigs that circumvent the rules.
5. **Remember:** the holster is **equipment**; mid-match changes may require re-check.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **Chapter 5** + division **Appendix**; cross-check **8.1** (holstered state before **Make Ready**).

## Common mistakes

- **Weak retention** — **10.5.3** risk if the gun falls during movement.
- **Holster higher** on the belt than allowed.
- **RO unaware** of division rules and waves through obvious breaches.
