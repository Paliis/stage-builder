## What this is

**Sections 5.1–5.8** of **IPSC Handgun** cover **handgun** requirements (configuration, allowed modifications, trigger, magwell, etc.) and **ammunition** (bullet type, minimum energy / PF after chrono, banned constructions). The bay **RO** catches **obvious** breaches; exact thresholds and measurement belong to **chrono** and **division arbiters** per **MD**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Visually check** division fit (compensator, optics, magazine length — **Chapter 5** and Appendix).
2. **Do not start** on clearly wrong ammunition (e.g. obvious calibre mismatch) — **RM**.
3. **Send to chrono** after powder/bullet changes if regs require a PF re-check.
4. **Safety first:** cracked frame, damaged chamber — **RM**, possible shooting halt.
5. **Do not substitute** technical rulings for the division arbiter.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Read **5.1–5.8** as a block; cross-link **10.5** / **10.4** if the issue becomes unsafe.

## Local (FPSU)

Range-specific ammo rules (steel, distance) — **MD** + FPSU regs. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Starting with a magazine** illegal for the division.
- **Ignoring** visibly damaged guns.
- **RO measures PF** without qualification and without a calibrated chrono.
