## What this is

The **Shotgun Jan 2026** rulebook includes a **popper calibration challenge** procedure in its **target / scoring appendices** (the internal matrix uses **Handgun Appendix C1** as a planning hint — **locate the exact appendix in the Shotgun PDF**). It covers disputes when a **popper** (or discipline-equivalent reactive steel, per PDF) fails to fall as expected, **RM** authority, evidence, and any **test** rules. **Shotgun** target systems and energy differ — never adjudicate from **Handgun** pages alone.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop** on-bay debates; record **facts** and point to the **Shotgun** appendix once verified.
2. **Preserve targets and scores** until **RM** / **CRO** clears changes.
3. **Do not** adjust popper springs or props without **RM** authorisation.
4. **Give neutral facts** if **RM** interviews you.
5. **On mixed ranges**, confirm the match is scored under **Shotgun** rules before citing any appendix.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Find the **popper calibration** appendix in **Shotgun**; tie to **Chapter 9** / equipment chapters as your PDF references.

## Local (FPSU)

Appeal forms may be informal at club level — **RM** authority still applies for calibration disputes. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating** shotgun steel disputes like **handgun** popper text without opening **Shotgun**.
- **Moving** props “to prove a point” before **RM** arrives.
- **Mixing** major-match video review customs with **Level I** appendix requirements.
