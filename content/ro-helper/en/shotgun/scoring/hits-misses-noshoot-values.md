## What this is

**9.4** **IPSC Shotgun** defines how **hits**, **misses**, and **no-shoot** (where used) contribute to the **stage / match score** for **Shotgun** COFs. Target presentations and scoring units may differ from pistol sports — follow **Shotgun** **Jan 2026** and the match’s **appendices**. **C170** = handgun reference only.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** the outcomes the **WSB** lists (e.g. hits on plates, misses, penalty targets) — **9.4** assigns their values.
2. **No-shoot / penalty targets:** if present on the stage, log every strike per **9.4** — do not normalize “to feel fair.”
3. **Steel / aerial / specialty:** use the stage briefing + **Chapter 9 Shotgun** — don’t import handgun tables.
4. **Squadding flow:** keep inputs consistent so **scoring** can apply **9.4** across many stages.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating Shotgun** like **handgun** paper scoring — different target systems and **9.4** tables.
- **Under-reporting** misses on multi-target arrays — breaks aggregate **9.4** totals.
