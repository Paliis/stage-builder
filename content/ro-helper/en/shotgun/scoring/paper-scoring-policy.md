## What this is

**9.5** **IPSC Shotgun** covers **paper** (or card) targets when **Shotgun** uses them. Many **COFs** mix **steel** and **paper** — **9.5** tells you how to score **paper** hits, **lines**, **no-shoot** contacts, **FTE**, etc., for **Shotgun** targets. **C175** Handgun is **not** a substitute. Follow **Shotgun** **Jan 2026** and the match **appendices**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read** the **WSB**: which **targets** are **paper** vs **steel** / **special** on this stage.
2. **Approach** paper only when **safe** and per **stage** flow (often after squad clear).
3. **Score** each **paper** target per **9.5 Shotgun** — no handgun overlay templates unless **MD** says they apply.
4. **Keep** the **shot sequence** / **penalty** logic clear if **9.5** interacts with **steel** on the same stage.
5. **Disputes** — **9.6**; **RM/CRO** for unresolved calls.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **9.5 Shotgun** + **9.4**; check **Chapter 9** cross-links for mixed-target **COFs**.

## Common mistakes

- **Importing** handgun **paper** scoring habits **without** opening **Shotgun** **9.5**.
- **Scoring steel** as paper **or** missing **no-shoot** on paper **because** the stage is “mostly steel.”
