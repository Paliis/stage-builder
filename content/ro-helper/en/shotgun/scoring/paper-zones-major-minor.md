## What this is

When **IPSC Shotgun** uses **paper** (or card) targets, **A / C / D** zones and **Major / Minor** multipliers follow **9.5 Shotgun** and the **Shotgun** target **appendices** — not **Handgun** diagrams. **C180** is **Handgun**-only reference. **Steel-heavy** stages still need **correct paper calls** wherever paper appears.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **From the WSB**, know which **paper** models appear and whether **Major/Minor** applies to that **division**.
2. **Score** each **paper** hole with **9.5 Shotgun** boundaries — use **Shotgun** overlays if **MD** mandates them.
3. **Keep** paper and **steel** results separate on the sheet so **scoring** applies **9.4**/**9.5** correctly.
4. **No** borrowing handgun **mini** zone templates unless the **appendix** says they are the same.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **9.5 Shotgun** + tables; **9.4 Shotgun** for values.

## Common mistakes

- **Shotgun** paper scored with **pistol** muscle memory instead of **Shotgun** **9.5**.
- **Skipping** zone letters because “it’s mostly steel” — breaks **Major/Minor** totals.
