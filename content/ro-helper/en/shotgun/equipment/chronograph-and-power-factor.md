## What this is

**5.6** and **5.7** govern **chronograph** use and **power factor (PF)** for **Shotgun**: when testing happens, shot count, thresholds, **low PF**, retests, etc. (exact division labels — per **Shotgun** rulebook). The bay **RO** usually does not run chrono — but must know a competitor **without valid PF** must not proceed per **MD** / division rules, and where to **redirect** after the **chrono official** rules. **C245** = **Handgun** — use the **Shotgun** PDF for **Chapter 5** numbers.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Check** the board / list for a **valid** chrono result for the current match day / phase.
2. **Hold the start** if the MD runs “no chrono, no shoot” and there is no record.
3. **Do not debate** sensors and temperature — that is **chrono** + **RM** territory.
4. **After ammo changes**, if regs require, send for **re-chrono** **before** further strings.
5. **Document** incidents (suspected ammo swapping) for **RM**.

## Common mistakes

- **Allowing shooting** without an official chrono entry when the match requires one.
- **RO shoots someone else’s shotgun** for PF without procedure.
- **Ignoring** mandatory re-chrono after a loadout change.
- Using **Handgun** **5.6–5.7** text **without** opening the **Shotgun** rulebook.
