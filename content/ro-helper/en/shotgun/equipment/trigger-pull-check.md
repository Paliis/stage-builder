## What this is

**Shotgun Jan 2026** defines **equipment** rules for divisions and appendices that may include **trigger** or **release** force checks **where the PDF requires them**. **Shotgun** mechanisms (release triggers, disconnectors, etc.) differ greatly from **handgun** and **rifle**; some divisions may **not** use a simple “kg on the shoe” test. **Read the Shotgun book** — do not assume the **Handgun Production** pull-weight story applies one-to-one.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Treat** formal gauge checks as **equipment inspection** / **RM** unless **MD** assigns qualified staff.
2. **Do not improvise** pull tests; unauthorised methods create protests.
3. **Stop** the line for mechanical unsafety visible during make-ready — **RM**.
4. **Ask** which **division** and **appendix** govern the gun before discussing legality.
5. **Escalate** disputes after an official check to **RM**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Find **Chapter 5** + relevant **appendices** in **Shotgun** for divisions that mandate trigger/release verification.

## Common mistakes

- **Importing Handgun D4 §6** numbers to **Shotgun** without opening **Shotgun**.
- **Bay RO “certifying”** release weight verbally.
- **Ignoring** that some **Shotgun** divisions rely on different compliance tests than pull weight.
