## What this is

**Failure to engage (FTE)** addresses mandatory targets or arrays not engaged as required by **COF** / **WSB**, with **penalty** under **10.2.7** and **scoring** under **9.5.6** in **Shotgun Jan 2026**. **Shotgun** scoring (steel knockdown, paper, mixed arrays) differs from handgun — still use **Shotgun** **Chapters 9–10** jointly; do not import another discipline’s tables.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** which plates / paper / clays were mandatory per **WSB** and what was actually shot.
2. **Apply** **9.5.6** + **10.2.7** per verified **Shotgun** text.
3. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
4. **Document** array name and target ID for multi-bay stages.
5. **On mixed matches**, confirm discipline before citing clauses.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.2.7** — penalty anchor for FTE-style faults in **Shotgun**.
- **9.5.6** — scoring anchor; read together with **10.2.7**.

## Local (FPSU)

Local target naming may differ — tie notes to **WSB** labels. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Handgun Chapter 9** habits on **shotgun** steel.
- **Penalty without** score correction per **9.5.6**.
- **Missing array context** on the scoresheet.
