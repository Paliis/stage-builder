## What this is

A **procedural penalty** applies for **COF** / **WSB** procedure breaches when **Chapter 10** does not give a more specific exclusive rule. **Shotgun Jan 2026** uses **10.1** as the general frame (read **10.1.1–10.1.2**). **Shotgun** COF features (loading ports, ammunition on belt, movement with long gun) create procedural issues — still map them to the **correct specific clause** when one exists before defaulting to **10.1**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record facts:** WSB condition, timing in the **COF**, and whether a **specific 10.2.x** fits better.
2. **Apply** scoresheet entries per **10.1** + any specific rule.
3. **Keep the squad safe** first — procedural calls never justify unsafe hurry.
4. **Brief briefly**; protests per **9.6**.
5. **Document** clearly (which array, which loading rule, which movement segment).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.1** — **Shotgun** general procedural frame; verify **10.1.2** point value.
- **Caps** — **10.2.3** when applicable in **Shotgun** PDF.

## Local (FPSU)

Match-specific loading tables may add local procedure — must not contradict **DQ** rules. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Defaulting to 10.1** when a **shotgun-specific** **10.2.x** already assigns the penalty.
- **Handgun point values** memorised instead of **Shotgun** opened.
- **Vague scoresheet notes** on multi-array stages.
