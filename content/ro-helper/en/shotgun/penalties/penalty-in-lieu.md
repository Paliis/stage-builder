## What this is

**10.2.10** describes when, instead of **mandatory completion** of a **COF** element (impossibility, broken stage, injury, etc. — **per the PDF**), a **penalty may be applied by decision of the Range Master (RM)**. This is **not** an informal deal on the line between RO and competitor: authority, announcement procedure, and **scoresheet** entries are defined by **Jan 2026 Shotgun** rules — read **Shotgun** **Chapter 10**, not **Handgun** alone.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Do not** assign “penalty in lieu” yourself — only after explicit **RM** (or match-delegated) decision in the form required by the PDF / match briefing.
2. **Record facts:** what cannot be done safely or fairly; time; witnesses.
3. **Hand off to RM** in a structured way (avoid blame narratives in the scoring record).
4. **After RM decision** — follow instructions to continue or stop the run and **scoresheet** per **10.2.10**.
5. **Brief the competitor** briefly; challenges per **9.6** where applicable.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.2.10** in **Shotgun**; read the full sub-rule and applicability.
- If the case is closer to **safety stop** or **stage rebuild**, other sections may apply — do not force **10.2.10** without a PDF basis.

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU match bulletins may detail RM escalation and documentation; follow them. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **RO “makes a deal”:** exceeds authority and match integrity.
- **No RM decision on record:** scoring cannot be reconstructed.
- **Confusing with DNF / reshoot:** different mechanisms — different rules.
