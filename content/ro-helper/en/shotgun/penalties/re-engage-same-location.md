## What this is

When the **WSB** requires movement between **shooting locations** / areas or arrays, the rules may forbid **again** engaging targets **from the same** position or area from which fire was already applied, if that yields an unfair advantage or contradicts the stage design. In **Shotgun** this is normally captured under **10.2.9** — exact conditions, exceptions, and **procedural** count **only** from the **Jan 2026 Shotgun** PDF and the **WSB** text. Read **Shotgun** **Chapter 10**, not **Handgun** alone.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read the WSB:** mandatory order of positions / arrays; any explicit ban on “return” fire from the same spot.
2. **Log sequence:** from which area shots were fired at each target / group; whether fire was re-applied after a position change.
3. **Check the PDF:** whether the episode fits **10.2.9** rather than another clause (e.g. **10.2.2** as pure WSB position without re-engage logic).
4. **Scoresheet** per **10.2.9** after verification.
5. **If the WSB is ambiguous** — **RM** before or after the string per match policy.
