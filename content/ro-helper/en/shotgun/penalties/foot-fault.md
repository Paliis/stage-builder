## What this is

A **foot fault** is **out-of-area ground contact** or a **fault-line** breach during the **COF** contrary to the **WSB** or general line rules. **Shotgun Jan 2026** anchors this under **10.2.1** (see **10.2.1.1** in your PDF). **Shotgun** movement (loading ports, array transitions, low ports) can change where feet land — still read **Shotgun** **10.2.1**, not another discipline’s layout habits.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** line identity, contact, timing vs shots for **10.2.1.1** if relevant.
2. **Confirm WSB** for active lines per array / movement segment.
3. **Apply** **10.2.1** / **10.2.1.1** per PDF — do not default to **10.1** when **10.2.1** governs.
4. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
5. **On multi-array stages**, note **which array** the fault occurred on.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Primary anchor: **10.2.1** in **Shotgun**; **10.2.1.1** from the same PDF only.

## Local (FPSU)

Clay / mixed practical layouts may use unusual fault geometry — **MD** diagram must align with **WSB**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Handgun foot-fault** mental picture on **shotgun** arrays without reading **Shotgun**.
- **Vague “foot fault”** notes without array / line / shot context.
- **Missing** that **10.2.1.1** may change counting when advantage is gained while faulting.
