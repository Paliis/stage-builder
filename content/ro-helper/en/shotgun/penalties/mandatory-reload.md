## What this is

A **mandatory reload** in the **WSB** means the competitor **must** perform a reload (when, how many shells, from which position or port, etc.) exactly as written. If the competitor **fails** that requirement, the penalty anchor is **10.2.4** in **IPSC Shotgun** — exact conditions and **procedural** count **only** from the **Jan 2026** PDF. **Shotgun** loading (tube, twin/quad, strong-hand rules in WSB) is discipline-specific — apply **Shotgun** **Chapter 10**, not handgun or rifle reload logic.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Read the WSB:** mandatory reload points; shell counts; any stated method or position; exceptions after malfunction if written.
2. **Record facts:** whether the required load/reload sequence happened; whether it matches the WSB (**per WSB and rules text only**).
3. **Apply the penalty:** scoresheet per **10.2.4** after PDF check — do not mis-bucket under **10.2.2** if the issue is reload, not position.
4. **Brief the competitor:** which WSB requirement was missed (**9.6** for challenges).
5. **Document:** WSB clause + moment in **COF** (including which array or position).

## Common mistakes

- **Applying** when the WSB does **not** mandate a reload at that point.
- **Vague sheet:** “no reload” without quoting the WSB line.
- **Wrong bucket:** do not force **10.2.4** when the PDF supports a different rule.
