## What this is

**10.5.14** in **Shotgun Jan 2026** addresses **ammunition** in the **Safety Area** — usually **DQ** for the same isolation and **AD**-prevention reasons as other disciplines. **Shotgun** adds context: **live shells** in pockets, shell caddies, and near the loading / unloading area are frequent issues. What counts as **ammunition** versus allowed **dummy** / **snap-cap** use is **only** in the **Shotgun** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** that live shells do not appear in the **Safety Area** during gun handling, including on tables and in competitor bags inside the marked zone.
2. **Stop** the breach verbally; if the competitor refuses to clear it — **RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.14** after confirmation.
3. **Do not grab** someone else’s shells — direct the owner; if unsafe — **CEASE FIRE** per protocol.
4. **Brief newcomers** on **open bolt / chamber flag** expectations if the match uses them — without contradicting **10.5.14** zone rules.
5. **Do not confuse** **10.5.14** with other loading / movement rules elsewhere in the book; open the right sub-clauses.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating** shotgun **Safety Area** like a casual “loading table” behind the squad.
- **RO carries** live shells while coaching inside the zone.
- **Ignoring** a single shell on a chair because “it’s not in the gun”.
