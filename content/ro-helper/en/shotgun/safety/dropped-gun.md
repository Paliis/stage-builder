## What this is

A **dropped firearm** under **IPSC Shotgun** means the shotgun **unintentionally released** and contacts the ground / stage during the **COF** or as **10.5.3** defines — typically **DQ**. Shell caddies and loading equipment are **not** the firearm; if only shells / carriers fall, check whether **10.5.3** applies or **other** rules. **C120** = handgun.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **STOP**; clear feet from the drop zone; **muzzle** direction first.
2. **No pickup** until **RM** / line protocol — open action rules for shotguns vary by stage briefing.
3. **Record:** pump vs. semi-auto; barrel / stock impact; whether any round fired (**10.4**).
4. **DQ** under **10.5.3** with **RM** confirmation if required.
5. **Range damage** — props, steel stands — note for MD.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.5.3 Shotgun**; read with **Ch.10** loading / handling rules in the same edition.

## Common mistakes

- **DQ for dropped shells** or a **loader** — confirm **firearm** vs. accessories in the PDF.
- **Rushing** the competitor to grab the gun before **bolt open / safe** verification.
