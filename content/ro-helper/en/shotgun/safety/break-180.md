## What this is

Under **IPSC Shotgun**, **10.5.2** enforces **muzzle safe angles** / the **180°** concept: the muzzle must remain **within the safe sector** for the stage, berms, and **WSB**. Breaking that plane is a **critical safety violation** and is normally **DQ** — confirm exact language in the **Shotgun** PDF (**Jan 2026**). For other disciplines’ phrasing of the same anchor, see **C105**–**C108**; on shotgun courses, use **Shotgun** rules only.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop:** Immediate **“STOP!”** / cease-fire per MD.
2. **Control:** Safe approach; competitor opens action / shows safe per **WSB** and division (open bolt, empty chamber rules).
3. **Unload / make safe:** Follow **Shotgun** match procedure and **RM** direction after the violation.
4. **DQ path:** Under **10.5.2**, document **DQ** on the **scoresheet** once confirmed against the PDF; notify **RM**.
5. **Witnesses / geometry:** Note where the **muzzle** was relative to the 180° / **WSB** boundary (ports, loading zones, movement paths).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Study **10.5.2** in **Shotgun** together with stage movement, **load / unload** commands, and **port** work — competitors often rotate the body while loading; the **muzzle** can cross the line **before** the eyes track.

## Common mistakes

- **Watching shells** or hands instead of **muzzle** during quad / twin loads.
- **Calling “DQ” from echo** — confirm **who** fired and **where** the muzzle pointed.
- **Assuming** shotgun exceptions match **rifle/PCC** wording — read **Shotgun** **10.5.2**.
