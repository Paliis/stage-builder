## What this is

In **Shotgun Jan 2026**, **10.5.11** governs **external safety** behaviour for **shotguns during movement** — when a manual or cross-bolt safety (if present) must be **engaged/disengaged**, in which **course segments**, and what constitutes a **DQ**. Many shotguns differ mechanically from rifles; **only** the **Shotgun** rulebook defines the obligation. **Handgun 10.5.11** remains trigger-finger language — do not substitute.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** safety state during **movement**, **loading ports**, and **ready / unload** sequences per **Chapter 8** and the **WSB**.
2. **Stop** the competitor on a clear **10.5.11** breach; involve **RM** for **DQ** when required after fact confirmation.
3. **Do not invent** “always off safe on shotgun” customs that contradict the published text.
4. **Separate** this from **action open / chamber flag** procedures if the match uses them — they live in other rules, not as a replacement for **10.5.11**.
5. **On mixed bays**, state explicitly which discipline’s book applies.

## Common mistakes

- **Using Handgun** muscle memory for **shotgun** movement.
- **Treating** “bolt back” as a substitute for whatever **10.5.11** requires for safety selector state.
- **Letting** squad folklore override the PDF.
