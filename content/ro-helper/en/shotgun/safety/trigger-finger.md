## What this is

**Chapter 10.5** **Shotgun** defines **trigger-finger discipline** during loading, movement between arrays, and when **not** engaging targets, per **Shotgun** **Jan 2026**. Twin / quad loads and port work can hide the guard — the **test** is still in **10.5.x** of the **Shotgun** PDF (matrix cites **10.5.9–10.5.11** as **Handgun** shorthand — **verify**). **C115** = handgun topic only.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Position** to see trigger guard during **load** sequences and **movement** — may require shifting with MD approval.
2. **Separate** trigger issues from **10.5.2** (180°) and **10.4** (AD) — different fact patterns.
3. **Stop** the squad if a **DQ**-level breach is observed; **RM** per regs.
4. **Note** whether the competitor’s hands were on shells vs. controls when the finger entered the guard.

## Common mistakes

- **Only watching** muzzle during fast loads — **finger** can enter the guard while the muzzle looks “OK”.
- **Calling DQ** from one RO who could not see the guard — need **evidence** or corroboration.
