## What this is

**Unauthorized loading** means loading or reloading **outside** the RO commands / stage conditions permitted by the rules (for example before **“Load”** or **“Make Ready”**, or outside the allowed zone/procedure). The rulebook normally treats confirmed **10.5.12** breaches as **DQ**—verify in your edition.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop** the unsafe act immediately (commands, **muzzle** control).
2. **Establish facts:** what the competitor did (magazine, chambered round, shotgun shells, etc.) and **when** relative to match commands.
3. **Compare to the PDF:** if it matches **10.5.12** (or the discipline equivalent), **DQ**; record the rule reference on the **scoresheet**.
4. **RM** per match protocol; after DQ the competitor is **out** of further courses of fire.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Confirm the equivalent clause for **long gun** in the Jan 2026 PDF; Handgun review anchor: **10.5.12**.

- ROs must know the **command sequence** for that stage (including the **WSB**)—otherwise you cannot fairly separate permitted loading from a breach.

## Common mistakes

- Treating **unauthorized loading** the same as a **procedural** reload issue when the rulebook assigns them to **different** clauses.
- Failing to record **when** the act happened relative to RO commands.
