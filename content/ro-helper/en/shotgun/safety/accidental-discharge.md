## What this is

An **accidental discharge (AD)** under **IPSC Shotgun** is defined in **Section 10.4** of the **Shotgun** **Jan 2026** PDF. A discharge **outside** the controlled **COF** sequence on **RO** command, or in other **listed** circumstances, is normally **DQ**. **C110** is the **handgun** parallel for topic only — use **Shotgun** **10.4** on shotgun courses (load ports, open actions, etc., per that book).

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Cease fire / STOP**; maintain stand-off; **muzzle** and **action** state matter for shotguns.
2. **Freeze** competitor gun handling until **RM** / line protocol clears it.
3. **Record:** which tube / chamber was involved, loading vs. engagement phase, witnesses, audio (if policy).
4. **DQ** per **10.4** after **RM** confirmation if required.
5. **RM** for range repair / target damage and stats.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Full **10.4** in **Shotgun**; read sub-clauses about when a discharge counts as AD vs. other Ch.10 topics.

## Common mistakes

- **Calling “AD”** when the rulebook treats the event under a **different** 10.x paragraph — read **10.4** carefully.
- **No photo / sketch** of impact point when berms or props are involved — helps **RM** review.
