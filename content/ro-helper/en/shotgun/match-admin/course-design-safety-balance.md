## What this is

**1.1** states **principles** for IPSC courses: **safety**, **fairness** across divisions, **balance** of shooting vs movement, etc. **ROs** rarely **design** stages but **must** stop obvious hazards or impossible **WSB** execution and report to **CRO/RM** **before** squads shoot. **C305** = **Handgun** — use the **Shotgun** PDF for **1.1**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **During stage inspection**, check firing zones, minimum distances, prop stability — within **MD** competence.
2. **Do not start** a squad if there is **acute** danger (broken brace, target shifted into RO arc).
3. **Document** photo/description for **RM** if the stage conflicts with **1.1** or the **WSB**.
4. **Feed back** to **MD** (via **CRO**) on unreadable WSB text or impossible mandatory elements.
5. **During the match**, do not “fix” design alone — only per **RM** command.

- **1.1 Shotgun** as framing; concrete steel / clay rules — **10.4.7** and other **Chapter 10** rules in the **Shotgun** PDF.

## Local (FPSU)

FPSU may add local minimum distances; they must appear in **WSB/MD**. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **“We’ll sort it live”** instead of stopping an unsafe stage.
- **RO moves** a target without **RM**.
- **Ignoring** multiple RO reports about the same hazard.
- Using **Handgun 1.1** **without** opening the **Shotgun** PDF.
