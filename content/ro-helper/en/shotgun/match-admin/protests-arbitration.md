## What this is

**Chapter 11** governs **protests** against official acts and **arbitration** for **Shotgun**. This is **not** the same as **9.6** (target scoring challenges) — though a competitor may walk **multiple steps** in practice. An **RO** is **not** on the jury and does **not** issue match-level final rulings. **C290** = **Handgun** — use the **Shotgun** PDF for **Chapter 11**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Explain the route:** where to file, by when, in what form — per **Chapter 11** and **MD**.
2. **Preserve evidence** (targets, sheet, video) per **RM**; do not alter the stage without permission.
3. **Do not pressure** a competitor to withdraw a lawful protest.
4. **Give factual testimony** if the jury calls you as a witness.
5. **Respect** the jury outcome after the process — no public “they got it wrong”.

## Common mistakes

- **RO “settles”** a protest to avoid paperwork.
- **Destroying evidence** before the jury convenes.
- **Threatening** a competitor for using protest rights.
- Using **Handgun Ch.11** **without** opening the **Shotgun** PDF.
