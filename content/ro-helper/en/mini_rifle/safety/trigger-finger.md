## What this is

**Chapter 10.5** **Mini Rifle** covers **trigger-finger discipline** for juniors and equipment-limited divisions — same **safety** expectation: finger **outside** the guard when the **COF** phase requires it, per **Mini Rifle** **Jan 2026** PDF. Confirm **10.5.9–10.5.11** (or the equivalent headings) in **that** book — matrix uses **Handgun** numbering as a **hint** only. **C115** = handgun parallel.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Clear view** of the guard; smaller rifles can be harder to see — use help from the squad / **CRO** ethically.
2. **Coaching proximity:** do not let noise replace **rule** evidence — still need **visible** breach per **10.5.x**.
3. **DQ** protocol with **RM** if minors are involved — follow **MD** / federation child-safety steps.
4. **Document** for protests: phase, position, witnesses.

## Common mistakes

- **Lower standard** “because they’re young” — safety rules are not relaxed in the PDF.
- **Confusing** trigger discipline with **equipment** fit problems — different issues.
