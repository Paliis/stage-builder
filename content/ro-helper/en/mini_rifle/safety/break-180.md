## What this is

The **180° / muzzle safe angles** rule in **IPSC Mini Rifle** requires the muzzle to stay **within the safe sector** for the stage and range. Crossing that boundary is a **critical safety violation**. Cross-discipline anchors: **C105** (handgun), **C106** (PCC), **C107** (rifle) — on mini rifle matches, apply the **Mini Rifle** PDF.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Stop:** Loud, clear **“STOP!”** (or match-standard cease-fire).
2. **Control:** Approach only if safe; cease fire; muzzle in a **safe** direction.
3. **Unload / clear:** Per **Mini Rifle** division rules and **WSB**.
4. **After safety:** Under **10.5.2**, muzzle-safe-angle breach is normally **DQ** — confirm in **IPSC Mini Rifle Competition Rules** (**Jan 2026**), **Chapter 10**.
5. **Report:** **RM**; scoresheet **“DQ — Rule 10.5.2 (180° / muzzle safe angles)”** with time and place.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

Read **10.5.2** in **Mini Rifle** for the full test during **COF**, including **movement**, **reloads**, and **malfunction** handling. Compact guns and young divisions do not relax the **muzzle** standard — verify **WSB** and supervision level per MD.

## Common mistakes

- **Judging intent** (“they were trying to be safe”) instead of **muzzle geometry**.
- **Low-ready / high port** transitions that **sweep** past the 180° line during footwork.
- **Using Rifle or PCC PDF** text by habit — wrong book for a **Mini Rifle** match.
