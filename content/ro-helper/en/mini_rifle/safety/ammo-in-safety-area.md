## What this is

**10.5.14** in **Mini Rifle Jan 2026** addresses **ammunition** in the **Safety Area** — usually **DQ** for the same safety reasons as other IPSC disciplines. **Mini rifle** setups are compact but still **long-gun** handling: magazines and loose rounds near the service area remain high-risk. Read the **Mini Rifle** PDF for what counts as **ammunition** and any **exceptions**; do not assume identical wording to **Handgun** or full-size **Rifle**.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Watch** that live rounds do not appear in the **Safety Area** during gun handling on tables / mats within the marked zone.
2. **Stop** the breach verbally; if the competitor refuses to clear it — **RM** for **DQ** under **10.5.14** after confirmation.
3. **Do not grab** someone else’s ammo — direct the owner; if unsafe — **CEASE FIRE** per protocol.
4. **Brief newcomers** on the **Safety Area** boundary; junior / club squads may need extra emphasis.
5. **Do not confuse** with allowed **empty magazine** rules if the PDF separates them.

## Common mistakes

- **Importing** handgun-only habits (holster talk) instead of **long gun** zone discipline.
- **RO carries** ammo while entering the gun-service zone.
- **Ignoring** a breach to avoid awkwardness.
