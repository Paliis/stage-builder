## What this is

In **Mini Rifle Jan 2026**, **10.5.11** addresses **external (manual) safety** for **long guns during movement** — when the selector must be **on/off**, during **specific commands**, and what constitutes a **DQ**. This is **not** the same content as **Handgun 10.5.11** (trigger finger). Compact rifles still follow **long gun** logic; read **Mini Rifle** wording, not **Rifle** or **PCC** by habit.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Observe** manual safety / selector state during **Make Ready**, **movement**, and **Unload / Show clear** per **WSB** + **Chapter 8** in your PDF.
2. **Stop** the competitor on a clear **10.5.11** breach; call **RM** for **DQ** when the rule mandates it after you confirm facts.
3. **Do not assume** junior / club squads “know” selector rules — brief explicitly.
4. **Separate** this from other **10.5.x** safety items (finger, AD context) per **Mini Rifle** numbering.
5. **On ranges** shared with **handgun**, repeat that **10.5.11** text differs by discipline.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.5.11** in **Mini Rifle**; read adjacent **10.5.x** in the same PDF.

## Common mistakes

- **Judging mini rifle** using **Handgun** PDF anchors.
- **Ignoring** a visibly wrong selector state during movement.
- **Mixing** junior equipment exceptions (if any in appendices) with **10.5.11** without **RM**.
