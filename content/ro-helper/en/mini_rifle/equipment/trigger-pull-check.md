## What this is

**Mini Rifle Jan 2026** defines **trigger pull** / **release force** checks for divisions that require them, via **Chapter 5** and **appendices** (the matrix reused **Handgun** planning anchors — **do not** copy kg values blindly). **Mini Rifle** is a distinct book from **Rifle** and **PCC**; junior / equipment nuances may differ. **Only** the **Mini Rifle** PDF is binding.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Treat** gauge checks as **equipment inspection** / **RM** unless **MD** assigns trained staff with correct tools.
2. **Do not** “eyeball” pass/fail for trigger weight on the bay.
3. **Stop** the line for obvious mechanical unsafety and call **RM** — separate from pull weight but urgent.
4. **Do not** assume **Handgun Production** numbers apply to **Mini Rifle** divisions without verification.
5. **Escalate** measurement disputes to **RM**.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Locate trigger-check rules under **Chapter 5** + relevant **appendices** in **Mini Rifle**; confirm which divisions require a pull test.

## Common mistakes

- **Using Rifle** appendix page numbers on a **Mini Rifle** match.
- **Bay RO certifying** trigger pull without authority.
- **Mixing** adult division thresholds with **junior** equipment rules without reading the appendix.
