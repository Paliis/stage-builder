## What this is

**10.2.12** in **IPSC Mini Rifle** addresses firing when the **fire control** or **discharge pattern** (e.g. **binary trigger**, **burst**, or **fully automatic** operation — **as defined in the PDF**) is **not permitted** for the competitor’s **division**, equipment rules, or **COF**. This card applies to **Mini Rifle** — always open the **Mini Rifle** PDF. For **PCC** / **Rifle**, see **C102** / **C103**. Outcomes (**procedural**, **DQ**, etc.) are **only** from **Jan 2026** text and match / division bulletins.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Confirm facts:** what was observed (multiple rounds per single deliberate pull, runaway burst, selector position, etc.) — **only** per match evidence policy and **safe** observation.
2. **Read 10.2.12** in **Mini Rifle Chapter 10** together with **Ch.5** equipment rules for the division.
3. **Apply the sanction** strictly per the PDF — do not substitute labels (“sounds like binary”) for rule text.
4. **Scoresheet / RM:** if classification or legality of the firearm is in doubt, escalate per match procedure.
5. **Safety first** if uncontrolled fire occurs — **RM** and range protocols before scoring debate.
