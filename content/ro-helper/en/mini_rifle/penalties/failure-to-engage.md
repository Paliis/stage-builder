## What this is

**Failure to engage (FTE)** covers mandatory targets not properly engaged per **COF** / **WSB**, with **penalty** under **10.2.7** and **scoring** under **9.5.6** in **Mini Rifle Jan 2026**. **Mini Rifle** is a separate book from **Rifle** — open **Chapter 9** and **Chapter 10** together in **that** PDF; junior stages do not waive the need to apply the correct clauses.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Record** mandatory targets, shots, and scoring outcomes per **WSB**.
2. **Apply** **9.5.6** + **10.2.7** after verifying tables and wording in **Mini Rifle**.
3. **Brief briefly**; challenges per **9.6**.
4. **Document** target labels and array for audits.
5. **If unsure** between FTE and another penalty, involve **CRO** / **RM** before signing.

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- **10.2.7** — penalty anchor for FTE-style faults in **Mini Rifle**.
- **9.5.6** — scoring anchor; joint read with **10.2.7**.

## Local (FPSU)

School/club simplifications must not erase required targets without **MD** authority aligned to rules tier. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Using Rifle** PDF because the gun “looks like” a full-size rifle.
- **Applying penalty** without adjusting score entries per **9.5.6**.
- **Vague sheet notes** for coaches reviewing junior scores.
