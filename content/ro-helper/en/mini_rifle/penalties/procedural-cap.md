## What this is

**10.2.3** limits **how many** times (or to what ceiling) **procedural** penalties may accrue in a defined context — e.g. per **shot**, **target**, **stage**, or another unit **exactly as the Jan 2026 Mini Rifle PDF states**. This is a **cap rule** for a specific class of violations — read **Mini Rifle** **Chapter 10**, not **Handgun** or full **Rifle** alone.

## What the Range Officer (RO) does

1. **Count procedurals** from **COF** facts under the applicable **10.2** / **10.1** clauses.
2. **Apply the cap:** if the total exceeds the **10.2.3** limit, **trim** scoring as the PDF requires (do not arbitrarily reduce without a rule basis).
3. **Scoresheet** clarity for scoring: how many episodes occurred vs how many **count** after the cap.
4. **If unsure** whether the same “string” of violations sits under one cap — **CRO/RM**.
5. **Avoid mixing** with multiple different clauses on one episode (depends on the PDF).

## IPSC (Jan 2026)

- Anchor: **10.2.3** in **Mini Rifle**; read together with the sub-rules the cap references.
- Match **chief scoring** / **RM** may issue uniform interpretation — follow match briefings.

If FPSU mirrors IPSC with deltas, the cap may differ; verify FPSU text. **`fpsu_delta_verified`**: **false** until **RO_HELPER_V0 §4.1** review.

## Common mistakes

- **Ignoring the cap:** extra procedurals on the scoresheet vs the PDF.
- **Wrong “container”:** cap per shot vs per target — category error.
- **Vague logging:** no episode breakdown — cannot audit **10.2.3**.
